Income Tax Summary Notes (AY 2025-26)

Based on Finance Act 2024 (FA 2024) Amendments

1. Introduction & Basics

  • Power to Levy: Central Govt levies Income Tax (other than on agricultural income). Power from Constitution, Art 246, Sch VII, Union List (List I), Entry 82. State Govt can tax agricultural income (State List II, Entry 46).
  • Income Tax Law Components: Income Tax Act 1961 (w.e.f 1-Apr-1962), Income Tax Rules, Circulars (Dept binding), Notifications (All binding), Case Laws (SC=Law of Land).
  • Finance Act 2024: Bill No. 2 presented July 23, 2024; Act received assent Aug 16, 2024. Applicable for AY 2025-26.
  • Charging Section (Sec 4): Tax levied in Assessment Year (AY) on income earned in the Previous Year (PY) at rates specified in the Finance Act.
  • PY & AY:
    • PY (FY 24-25): 1 Apr 2024 - 31 Mar 2025 (Year income earned). Can be < 12 months for new business/source.
    • AY (FY 25-26): 1 Apr 2025 - 31 Mar 2026 (Year income assessed/taxed). Always 12 months.
  • Person (Sec 2(31)): Individual, HUF, Company, Firm, LLP, AOP, BOI, Local Authority, AJP.
  • Assessee (Sec 2(7)): Any person liable to pay tax, interest, penalty, or any sum under the Act.

2. Tax Liability in Previous Year (Exceptions)

  • General Rule: Tax paid in AY.
  • Exceptions (Tax paid in PY itself):
    • Sec 172: Non-Resident Shipping Business (Income = 7.5% of freight from India, Tax @ 35%) - Mandatory.
    • Sec 174: Person leaving India permanently (If AO directs).
    • Sec 174A: AOP/BOI/AJP formed for specific purpose, purpose achieved (If AO directs).
    • Sec 175: Transfer of assets to avoid tax (If AO directs).
    • Sec 176: Discontinued Business (If AO directs).

3. Tax Rates & Surcharge (AY 2025-26 - Normal Regime)

Note: Section 115BAC is now the default regime, see Section 6. These rates apply if opting out of 115BAC.

  • Individual, HUF, AOP, BOI, AJP (Resident/Non-Resident):
    • Up to ₹2,50,000: Nil
    • ₹2,50,001 - ₹5,00,000: 5%
    • ₹5,00,001 - ₹10,00,000: 20%
    • Above ₹10,00,000: 30%
    • Senior Citizen (Resident, 60+ yrs): Basic exemption ₹3,00,000.
    • Super Senior Citizen (Resident, 80+ yrs): Basic exemption ₹5,00,000 (5% slab skipped).
    • (Birthday on 1st April counts for previous year completion)
  • Surcharge (Individual, HUF, etc.): On tax amount.
    • > ₹50L - ₹1 Cr: 10%
    • > ₹1 Cr - ₹2 Cr: 15%
    • > ₹2 Cr - ₹5 Cr: 25%
    • > ₹5 Cr: 37%
    • Max Surcharge 15% on tax from: LTCG 112A (Tax @12.5% > 1.25L), STCG 111A (Tax @20%), Dividend (Tax @ Slab), LTCG 112 (Tax @12.5%).
    • (If Total Income > 2 Cr, surcharge on tax from above special incomes capped at 15%, tax on other income gets 15%/25%/37% based on 'other income' level).
  • Company:
    • Domestic: 30%. (25% if PY 22-23 Turnover ≤ ₹400 Cr, including new companies).
    • Foreign: 35% (Reduced from 40%).
    • Surcharge (Company):
      • Domestic: >₹1 Cr - ₹10 Cr @ 7%; >₹10 Cr @ 12%.
      • Foreign: >₹1 Cr - ₹10 Cr @ 2%; >₹10 Cr @ 5%.
  • Partnership Firm, LLP, Local Authority:
    • Flat Rate: 30%.
    • Surcharge: >₹1 Cr @ 12%.
  • Co-operative Society (Normal Regime):
    • Up to ₹10k @ 10%; ₹10k-₹20k @ 20%; >₹20k @ 30%.
    • Surcharge: Like Domestic Co. (>₹1 Cr - ₹10 Cr @ 7%; >₹10 Cr @ 12%).
  • Health & Education Cess (HEC): 4% on (Tax + Surcharge). Applies to all assessees.
  • Rounding Off (Sec 288A/B): Total Income and Tax Payable rounded to nearest multiple of ₹10.

4. Reliefs & Rebates (Normal Regime)

  • Marginal Relief: Available if income is slightly above surcharge thresholds (50L, 1Cr, 2Cr, 5Cr for Indiv/HUF etc.; 1Cr, 10Cr for Co/Co-op; 1Cr for Firm). Relief ensures tax increase doesn't exceed income increase over the threshold. Calculation: `Tax Payable ≤ Tax on Threshold + (Income - Threshold)`.
  • Rebate u/s 87A (Normal Regime):
    • Eligible: Resident Individual.
    • Condition: Total Income ≤ ₹5,00,000.
    • Amount: 100% of tax or ₹12,500, whichever is lower.
    • Apply before HEC.
    • Not available against LTCG u/s 112A tax.
    • No separate marginal relief for the rebate threshold itself.

5. Alternative Tax Regimes (Optional / Specific Conditions)

  • Sec 115BAA (Domestic Companies):
    • Optional. Tax Rate 22% + Mandatory 10% Surcharge + 4% HEC (Eff: 25.168%).
    • No MAT. MAT Credit lapses.
    • Sacrifices: No 10AA, Addnl Dep, 33AB/ABA, 35(Donations), 35AD, 35CCC/CCD, most Chapter VI-A deductions (Except 80JJAA, 80LA, 80M). Related losses/depreciation lapse.
    • Option exercise via Form 10-IC by ITR due date. Once opted, generally cannot opt out.
  • Sec 115BAB (New Mfg Domestic Companies):
    • Conditions: Setup 1 Oct 19 - 31 Mar 24 start, New business, New P&M (mostly), specified mfg activities (exclusions apply, power gen is mfg), no hotel/convention bldg use.
    • Rate on Mfg Income: 15% + Mandatory 10% Surcharge + 4% HEC (Eff: 17.16%).
    • Other Incomes: Special rates apply (+10% sur); HP/OS @ 22% (+10% sur) on gross; STCG(Dep)@15%, STCG(Non-Dep)@22%.
    • No MAT. No MAT credit issue (new co).
    • Sacrifices: Similar to 115BAA (but 80M allowed, not 80LA).
    • Anti-abuse (Sec 115BAB(6)): AO can tax excess profits @ 30% (+10% sur).
    • Option via Form 10-ID with *first* ITR. Once opted, generally cannot opt out (Violation of specific conditions may allow shift to 115BAA).
  • Sec 115BAD (Resident Co-op Societies):
    • Optional. Tax Rate 22% + Mandatory 10% Surcharge + 4% HEC.
    • Mirrors 115BAA rules (No AMT, AMT credit lapse, similar sacrifices - 80JJAA/LA allowed).
  • Sec 115BAE (New Mfg Resident Co-op Societies):
    • Optional. Conditions: Setup 1 Apr 23 - 31 Mar 24 start.
    • Rate on Mfg Income: 15% + Mandatory 10% Surcharge + 4% HEC.
    • Mirrors 115BAB rules (No AMT, similar sacrifices - 80JJAA/M allowed).
    • Key Difference from 115BAB: No restriction on using building previously used as hotel/convention center.

6. Section 115BAC Regime (Default - Indiv/HUF/AOP/BOI/AJP)

  • Default Regime: Applies automatically unless opted out.
  • Tax Slabs (AY 25-26):
    Income Slab (₹)Rate
    Up to 3,00,000Nil
    3,00,001 - 7,00,0005%
    7,00,001 - 10,00,00010%
    10,00,001 - 12,00,00015%
    12,00,001 - 15,00,00020%
    Above 15,00,00030%
  • Surcharge (115BAC):
    • > ₹50L - ₹1 Cr: 10%
    • > ₹1 Cr - ₹2 Cr: 15%
    • > ₹2 Cr: 25% (No 37% surcharge under 115BAC).
    • Max 15% surcharge rule on special incomes (112A, 111A, Div, 112) still applies.
  • Marginal Relief: Applicable at surcharge thresholds (50L, 1Cr, 2Cr).
  • HEC: 4% applies.
  • No AMT. AMT Credit lapses.
  • Sacrifices (Key ones disallowed):
    • Salary: Entertainment Allce, Prof Tax, LTC, HRA, Most Allowances (except DTDC - Divyang Transport, Travel, Daily, Conveyance). Standard Deduction enhanced to ₹75,000.
    • House Property: Interest on SOP. LOP/DLOP loss cannot be set-off against other heads or carried forward.
    • PGBP/Other: 10AA, Addnl Dep, 33AB/ABA, 35(Donation), 35AD, 35CCC/CCD. Minor child exemption (1500). MP/MLA allowances.
    • Chapter VI-A: Most deductions (80C, D, E, TTA, TTB etc.). Allowed: 80JJAA, 80CCD(2), 80CCH(2), 80LA.
    • Related losses/depreciation lapse.
  • Opting Out: Must opt-out by ITR due date (cannot opt-out if filing late).
    • No Business Income: Choice available every year.
    • Business Income: If opt-out, remain out. If opt-in (or default applies), locked in while business income exists.
  • Rebate u/s 87A (under 115BAC):
    • Eligible: Resident Individual.
    • Condition: Total Income ≤ ₹7,00,000.
    • Amount: 100% of tax or ₹25,000, whichever is lower.
    • Marginal relief available for income slightly above 7L.

7. Other Special Tax Rates

  • Sec 115BB: Winnings (Lottery, Games etc.) @ 30%.
  • Sec 68-69D: Unexplained Cash Credits, Investments, Expenditure etc. @ 60% + Mandatory 25% Surcharge + 4% HEC (Effective Rate 78%).
  • Sec 115BBF: Royalty on Patents (Resident Inventor, conditions apply) @ Optional 10%.
  • Sec 115BBG: Income from Transfer of Carbon Credits @ 10%.
  • Sec 115BBJ: Net Winnings from Online Games @ 30%.
  • Limitations: Generally, no deduction for expenses, no Chapter VI-A deductions, and no basic exemption benefit against these special rate incomes (Exception: Res Indiv/HUF can set off unexhausted basic exemption against LTCG 112/112A, STCG 111A).

8. Diversion vs. Application of Income

  • Diversion by Overriding Title: Obligation attached to the source of income by law/deed. Income diverted before reaching assessee. Taxable in recipient's hands.
  • Application of Income: Assessee applies income after receipt due to an obligation. Taxable in assessee's (earner's) hands first.
