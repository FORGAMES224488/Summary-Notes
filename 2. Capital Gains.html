Capital Gains Summary

1. Charging Section (Sec 45(1))

Profit/gain from the transfer of a capital asset is taxed under 'Capital Gains' in the year the transfer occurs. Taxability arises upon transfer, irrespective of when consideration is received.

2. Capital Asset (Sec 2(14))

Definition: Property of any kind (movable/immovable, tangible/intangible), whether or not connected with business/profession.

Exclusions (Not Capital Assets):

  • Stock-in-trade (Taxed under PGBP).
  • Movable personal assets (e.g., furniture, car, mobile).
    • Exceptions (Treated as Capital Assets): Jewellery, drawings, paintings, sculptures, archaeological collections, any other work of art.
  • Rural agricultural land in India.
  • Gold Bonds 1999, Gold Deposit Bonds, Deposit Certificates under Gold Monetisation Scheme 2015.

Rural vs. Urban Agricultural Land:

  • Urban Area: Municipality with population ≥ 10,000. Includes area within:
    • 2 Kms if population > 10,000 ≤ 1 Lakh
    • 6 Kms if population > 1 Lakh ≤ 10 Lakh
    • 8 Kms if population > 10 Lakh
  • Land outside these limits is Rural (transfer is exempt).

ULIPs: Generally treated as capital assets (taxability depends on conditions, see Sec 10(10D)/45(1B)).

3. Transfer (Sec 2(47))

Includes: Sale, exchange, relinquishment, extinguishment of rights, compulsory acquisition, conversion to stock-in-trade, transfer of possession (immovable property in part performance), transfer of shares giving right to enjoy immovable property (co-op society), redemption of Zero Coupon Bonds (ZCBs).

4. Types of Capital Assets & Period of Holding (PoH)

Short-Term Capital Asset (STCA): Held for PoH ≤ specified limit.

Long-Term Capital Asset (LTCA): Held for PoH > specified limit.

Holding Periods:

  • 1 Year: For listed securities (shares, debentures, govt securities, derivatives, now including listed Units), Units of UTI, Units of Equity Oriented MF, ZCBs. (Post 23/07/2024: 'Other than unit' removed).
  • 2 Years (Post 23/07/2024): For Unlisted Shares, Immovable Property, and ALL other assets not covered in the 1-year category. (Replaces previous 2/3 year distinction for these).
  • 3 Years (Exception): Holding period for determining LTCG/STCG for Slum Sale undertakings (Sec 50B) remains 3 years, even post 23/07/2024 amendment.

PoH Start Date for Shares/Securities: Date of allotment.

5. Computation of Capital Gains (Sec 48)

Full Value of Consideration (FVC)
Less: Transfer Expenses
= Net Consideration
Less: Cost of Acquisition (COA) / Indexed COA (ICOA)
Less: Cost of Improvement (COI) / Indexed COI (ICOI)
= Capital Gain (LTCG or STCG)

Proviso: COA/COI shall not include amounts claimed as deduction under Sec 24(b) (Interest on Housing Loan) or Chapter VI-A (like 80C for stamp duty/registration on house purchase).

5.1. Indexation

Applies only to LTCG (except specified cases).

ICOA = COA * (CII of Year of Transfer / CII of Year of Acquisition)

ICOI = COI * (CII of Year of Transfer / CII of Year of Improvement)

Base Year: 2001-02 (CII = 100). CII for PY 2023-24 (AY 2024-25): 348. CII for PY 2024-25 (AY 2025-26): 363.

Indexation Removal (Amendment): Indexation benefit is removed for calculating capital gain amount for transfers on or after 23rd July 2024.

Optional Tax Calculation for Immovable Property: Resident Individuals/HUFs transferring immovable property (acquired before 23/07/24) on or after 23/07/24 can choose to pay tax at the lower of:

  • 20% with Indexation (calculated separately only for tax computation) OR
  • 12.5% without Indexation.
Note: Capital gain included in Total Income is always calculated *without* indexation post 23/07/24. The option is only for determining the *tax liability* on that gain.

Indexation Not Available (Even Before Amendment): Debentures/Bonds (except Capital Indexed Bonds & Sovereign Gold Bonds), Slum Sale (Sec 50B), LTCG under Sec 112A, Non-Resident gains under First Proviso to Sec 48.

5.2. COA for Assets Acquired Before 01/04/2001

COA = Higher of:

  • Actual Cost
  • FMV as on 01/04/2001 (but FMV cannot exceed SDV as on 01/04/2001 for immovable property).

This benefit of using FMV as on 01/04/2001 remains even after the removal of indexation.

COI before 01/04/2001: Ignored.

5.3. COA/COI for Specific Assets (Sec 55)

  • Intangible Assets (Goodwill, Trademark, Route Permit etc.):
    • Self-Generated: COA = Nil.
    • Purchased: COA = Purchase Price.
    • COI = Always Nil.
    • No FMV benefit as on 01/04/2001.
  • Bonus Shares:
    • Allotted on/after 01/04/2001: COA = Nil.
    • Allotted before 01/04/2001: COA = FMV as on 01/04/2001.
    • Subject to 112A acquired before 31/01/2018: Cost determined by grandfathering rule (Sec 112A).
  • Right Shares:
    • Subscribed by shareholder: COA = Amount paid to company.
    • Right Renounced by shareholder: Gain = Consideration received (Cost=Nil, always STCG).
    • Purchased by Renouncee: COA = Amount paid to company + Amount paid for right.

6. Special Provisions & Deeming Sections

6.1. Non-Resident CG Calculation (First Proviso to Sec 48)

For NR transferring shares/debentures of an Indian company acquired in foreign currency:

  1. Convert FVC, Expenses, COA to foreign currency using Average Rates (TTBR+TTSR)/2 of transfer/acquisition dates respectively.
  2. Calculate gain in foreign currency.
  3. Convert gain back to INR using TTBR on the date of transfer.
  4. No indexation benefit. Not applicable if Sec 112A applies.

6.2. Firm/Partner Transactions

  • Partner transfers asset to Firm (Sec 45(3)): FVC for partner = Amount recorded in firm's books.
  • Firm transfers asset/money to Partner (Dissolution/Reconstitution):
    • Sec 9B: If Firm transfers Capital Asset or Stock-in-Trade to partner on dissolution/reconstitution, Firm is taxed (CG or PGBP). FVC = FMV on date partner receives asset.
    • Sec 45(4): On Reconstitution only, if partner receives Money or Capital Asset, Firm taxed on Capital Gain = (Money + FMV of Capital Asset received) - Partner's Capital Balance (adjusted for 9B impact). Taxable gain cannot be negative. Attributed to remaining revalued assets.

6.3. Demat Shares (Sec 45(2A))

Use FIFO method based on date of credit to Demat account (not purchase date) for calculating cost. Apply FIFO separately for each Demat account.

6.4. LIP / ULIP Taxation (Key Changes)

  • General LIP (Sec 10(10D)): Maturity generally exempt. Taxable if:
    • Keyman Insurance Policy (Taxed under Salary/PGBP/IFOS based on recipient).
    • Premium exceeds % of Sum Assured (10%/15%/20% rule based on issue date).
    • Policy issued on/after 01/04/2023 & aggregate annual premium > ₹5 Lakh (Excl. Term Plan premium & GST). Exemption available for policies with aggregate premium up to ₹5L. Taxable amount (Receipts - Premiums paid [if 80C not claimed]) taxed under IFOS (Sec 56(2)(xiii)).
  • ULIPs (Sec 10(10D) / 45(1B)):
    • Considered capital asset.
    • Exemption under 10(10D) denied if issued on/after 01/02/2021 & aggregate annual premium > ₹2.5 Lakh. Exemption available for policies with aggregate premium up to ₹2.5L.
    • If taxable, gain (Receipts - Premiums paid) taxed under Capital Gains (Sec 45(1B)). Treated as LTCG under Sec 112A (No indexation).
  • Death proceeds remain exempt generally.
  • Term Plan premiums excluded from ₹5L limit check.
  • GST on premium excluded from ₹5L/₹2.5L limit check and from deductible premium amount.

6.5. Slum Sale (Sec 50B)

Transfer of an undertaking for a lump sum consideration without assigning values to individual assets/liabilities.

  • FVC: Higher of FMV1 (FMV of undertaking transferred) and FMV2 (FMV of consideration received), calculated as per Rule 11UAE.
  • COA: Net Worth of the undertaking (Assets - Liabilities).
    • Assets: Depreciable @ WDV (Income Tax Act), Others @ Book Value. Ignore revaluation.
    • Liabilities: @ Book Value (Exclude Share Capital, Reserves, Tax Provisions, Contingent Liabilities).
    • Nil value for self-generated goodwill & assets claimed under 35AD.
    • Net worth cannot be negative (take Nil if negative).
  • Indexation: Not available.
  • LTCG/STCG: Based on PoH of undertaking (>3 years = LTCG).
  • No separate PGBP calculation for stock transferred.

6.6. Deeming FVC Sections

  • Sec 50C: If SDV > 110% of apparent sale consideration for transfer of Land/Building (Capital Asset), then SDV is deemed FVC. Option to take SDV as on agreement date if conditions met. Assessee can dispute SDV and refer to VO.
  • Sec 50CA: If FMV > consideration for transfer of Unlisted Shares, then FMV is deemed FVC.
  • Sec 50D: If consideration is not ascertainable, FMV of the asset transferred is deemed FVC.

6.7. Advance Money Forfeited (Sec 51)

  • Forfeited before 01/04/2014: Reduce from COA/ICOA/WDV when the asset is eventually sold (only if forfeited by current owner).
  • Forfeited on/after 01/04/2014: Taxable under IFOS (Sec 56(2)(ix)) in the year of forfeiture.

7. Transactions Not Regarded as Transfer (Sec 47 - Key Examples)

No capital gains arise on these transfers. Cost and PoH for the recipient are generally taken from the previous owner.

  • Distribution of assets on HUF partition.
  • Gift, Will, Irrevocable Trust. (Amendment: Gift by Company/Firm is now generally taxable. Only gift by Individual/HUF remains exempt under Sec 47. Exception: Gift of ESOP/Sweat Equity is taxable even if by individual, FVC = FMV).
  • Transfer between Holding & 100% Indian Subsidiary (subject to Sec 47A conditions - 8-year lock-in).
  • Transfer of assets by amalgamating company to Indian amalgamated company.
  • Allotment of shares by amalgamated company to shareholders of amalgamating company.
  • Transfer of assets by demerged company to Indian resulting company.
  • Allotment of shares by resulting company to shareholders of demerged company.
  • Transfer of Bonds/GDRs/RDBs of Indian Co. or certain Govt Sec by NR to NR outside India.
  • Transfer of specified assets by NR on IFSC exchange for foreign currency.
  • Succession of Firm/Proprietorship into a Company (subject to conditions - lock-in, shareholding).
  • Conversion of private company/unlisted public company into LLP (subject to conditions - turnover limit, asset limit, lock-in).
  • Conversion of Bonds/Debentures into Shares/Debentures of the same company.
  • Conversion of Preference Shares into Equity Shares of the same company.
  • Conversion of Physical Gold to Electronic Gold Receipt (EGR) and vice-versa.
  • Redemption of Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs) by an Individual on maturity.
  • Transfer under Stock Lending Scheme.
  • Transfer under Reverse Mortgage Scheme.
  • Consolidation of MF Schemes/Plans (subject to conditions).

8. Capital Gains Exemptions (Sec 54 Series)

Invest the capital gain or net consideration (as applicable) within specified time limits to claim exemption. Investment must generally be made before the return filing due date, or amount deposited in Capital Gains Account Scheme (CGAS).

Common Points: CGAS applicable (except 54EC). Lock-in period for new asset (typically 3 years, 5 years for 54EC bonds). Violation leads to withdrawal of exemption. Max investment in new residential house property considered for exemption under 54 & 54F is capped at ₹10 Crore (from AY 2024-25).
  • Sec 54 (Individual/HUF): Transfer Residential House (LTCA). Invest LTCG in One Residential House in India (Two houses if LTCG ≤ ₹2 Cr, lifetime option). Time: -1 / +2 yrs (Purchase), +3 yrs (Construct).
  • Sec 54B (Individual/HUF): Transfer Urban Agricultural Land (used for agri for 2 yrs prior; STCA/LTCA). Invest CG in Agricultural Land (Urban/Rural). Time: +2 yrs.
  • Sec 54D (Any Assessee): Compulsory Acquisition of Industrial Land/Building (used for 2 yrs prior; STCA/LTCA). Invest CG in Industrial Land/Building. Time: +3 yrs from compensation receipt.
  • Sec 54EC (Any Assessee): Transfer Land/Building/Both (LTCA). Invest LTCG (Max ₹50 Lakh) in specified bonds (NHAI, RECL, PFCL, IRFCL) redeemable after 5 yrs. Time: +6 months. (Lock-in 5 yrs for bonds).
  • Sec 54F (Individual/HUF): Transfer Any LTCA (Other than Residential House). Invest Net Consideration in One Residential House in India. Time: -1 / +2 yrs (Purchase), +3 yrs (Construct). Exemption = CG * (Amt Invested / Net Consideration). Conditions: Own max 1 other house on transfer date, don't buy another within +2/+3 yrs.
  • Sec 54G (Any Assessee): Shifting Industrial Undertaking from Urban to Rural Area. Transfer Land/Building/P&M (STCA/LTCA). Invest CG in Land/Building/New P&M/Shifting Exp in Rural Area. Time: -1 / +3 yrs.
  • Sec 54GA (Any Assessee): Shifting Industrial Undertaking from Urban Area to SEZ. Transfer Land/Building/P&M (STCA/LTCA). Invest CG in Land/Building/P&M (New/Old)/Shifting Exp in SEZ. Time: -1 / +3 yrs.

9. Tax Rates Summary

  • STCG (Sec 111A - Listed Equity/Eq MF Units/Business Trust Units with STT): 20% (From 23/07/2024, was 15%).
  • Other STCG: Taxed at normal slab rates.
  • LTCG (Sec 112A - Listed Equity/Eq MF Units/Business Trust Units with STT): 10% (if transferred before 23/07/24) or 12.5% (if transferred on/after 23/07/24) on gain exceeding ₹1,25,000 (was ₹1L). No indexation. STT conditions apply. Grandfathering for assets bought before 01/02/2018.
  • LTCG (Sec 112 - Other LTCG): 12.5% (if transferred on/after 23/07/2024, was 20%). Indexation benefit removed for CG calculation post 23/07/24, but optional 20% with indexation available for tax calculation on immovable property for resident individuals/HUFs.
  • LTCG (Sec 112 - NR - Unlisted Securities): 12.5% (From 23/07/2024, was 10%) without indexation.

Basic Exemption Limit Adjustment: Resident Individuals/HUFs can adjust shortfall in basic exemption limit against LTCG (112/112A) and STCG (111A) after adjusting against other income.
