Chapter Notes: Transfer Pricing

1. Introduction & Charging Section

Transfer Pricing (TP) provisions aim to ensure that transactions between Associated Enterprises (AEs) are conducted at an Arm's Length Price (ALP), as if they were between unrelated parties.

Charging Section: Sec 92 mandates that income from an International Transaction between AEs must be computed having regard to the ALP.

Importance: Approx. 8-10 marks in CA Final / CMA; 30 marks in Elective Paper 6C.

2. Key Definitions

2.1. International Transaction (Sec 92B)

  • A transaction between two or more AEs.
  • At least one of the AEs must be a Non-Resident.
  • Includes transactions like sale/purchase of goods/services, lending/borrowing, cost allocation, intangibles, etc.
  • Deemed International Transaction: Transaction with an unrelated third party can be deemed an IT if:
    • There's a prior agreement between the AE and the third party regarding the transaction, OR
    • The terms are substantially determined by the AE.
  • Transactions between two non-residents can be IT if one NR's income is taxable in India.

2.2. Associated Enterprise (AE) (Sec 92A)

Two enterprises are AEs if, at any time during the previous year, one enterprise meets certain conditions with respect to the other. Key conditions include:

  • Direct/Indirect Participation: Management, control, or capital.
  • Shareholding: Holding ≥ 26% voting power directly or indirectly.
  • Common Control (Shares): One entity holds ≥ 26% voting power in both enterprises.
  • Loan: Loan advanced constitutes ≥ 51% of the borrower's total assets (book value).
  • Guarantee: Guarantee provided for ≥ 10% of the borrower's total borrowings.
  • Board Control: Appoints > half the BoD or 1 Executive Director.
  • Common Board Control: One entity appoints > half BoD or 1 Exec Dir in both enterprises.
  • Dependence on Intangibles: Business wholly dependent on know-how, patents, etc., owned by the other.
  • Raw Material Supply: ≥ 90% raw material supplied by one entity, influencing price.
  • Buyer Influence: Buyer dictates or influences prices significantly.
  • Individual/HUF Control: Controlled by the same individual/relative or HUF/member/relative.
  • Firms/AOP/BOI: Interest of ≥ 10% in profits/capital/beneficial interest.

3. Computation of Arm's Length Price (ALP) (Sec 92C)

ALP must be determined using the Most Appropriate Method (MAM).

3.1. Prescribed Methods:

  1. Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP): Compares price in AE transaction with price in a comparable uncontrolled transaction. Requires adjustments for functional differences (e.g., CIF/FOB, support, volume discounts, credit terms). Starts with Uncontrolled Price.
  2. Resale Price Method (RPM): Used typically for distributors. Starts with Resale Price to unrelated party, reduces normal GP margin & purchase expenses to arrive at ALP (purchase price from AE).
  3. Cost Plus Method (CPM): Used typically for manufacturers/service providers. Starts with Cost (direct & indirect), adds normal GP mark-up to arrive at ALP (selling price to AE). Adjust GP for differences (e.g., AE provides support/know-how).
  4. Profit Split Method (PSM): Used for highly integrated transactions/joint ventures. Splits combined profit based on relative contributions (FAR analysis: Functions, Assets, Risks).
  5. Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM): Compares net profit margin (relative to cost/sales/assets) from AE transaction with margin from comparable uncontrolled transactions or comparable independent companies. Often used when CUP/RPM/CPM are difficult.

Note: TP provisions do not apply if applying ALP reduces taxable income or increases loss in India.

3.2. Multiple ALPs & Range Concept

  • Less than 6 ALPs: Calculate the average ALP. If the difference between Actual Transaction Price (ATP) and Average ALP is within ±3% of ATP (or ±1% for wholesale trading), ATP is deemed ALP. Otherwise, Average ALP is the ALP.
  • 6 or more ALPs (Range Concept): (Not applicable for PSM)
    1. Arrange ALPs in ascending order.
    2. Determine the 35th and 65th percentile values to form the range.
      • If percentile calculation results in non-whole number (e.g., 2.45), use the next whole number position (e.g., 3rd).
      • If it results in a whole number (e.g., 7), use the average of the values at that position and the next position (e.g., average of 7th and 8th).
    3. If ATP falls within the range, ATP is the ALP.
    4. If ATP falls outside the range, the median (50th percentile) of the dataset is the ALP (calculated using same whole/non-whole number logic).
  • Multi-Year Data: For RPM, CPM, TNMM, if multiple year data (current + preceding 2 years) is available, use a weighted average of the comparable data (margins/percentages), weighted by relevant base (sales for RPM, cost for CPM, cost/sales/assets for TNMM). Calculate average margins first, then apply range concept if needed.

3.3. Assessing Officer (AO) determining ALP (Sec 92C(3))

AO can determine ALP if assessee fails to:

  • Apply MAM.
  • Use reliable data.
  • Maintain/furnish prescribed documentation (92D).

If AO enhances income via ALP adjustment: No Chapter VIA / Sec 10AA deduction on enhanced income. AE's income remains unaffected.

4. Procedural Aspects

4.1. Reference to Transfer Pricing Officer (TPO) (Sec 92CA)

  • AO may refer ALP computation to TPO (with CIT/PCIT approval).
  • TPO has powers to gather information, conduct inquiry.
  • TPO's order is binding on the AO.
  • TPO must pass order at least 60 days before AO's time limit for assessment completion.
  • Process becoming faceless.

4.2. Safe Harbour Rules (SHR) (Sec 92CB)

  • CBDT can notify rules where declared price/margin is accepted (reduces disputes).
  • Currently rules are not notified for exams.

4.3. Advance Pricing Agreement (APA) (Sec 92CC & 92CD)

Agreement between CBDT and assessee determining ALP methodology/specific ALP for future international transactions (not SDT).

  • Duration: Max 5 consecutive years.
  • Binding: On assessee and tax authorities (up to CIT/PCIT level), unless change in law/facts or fraud/misrepresentation (can be declared void ab initio).
  • Rollback: Option to apply APA to max 4 preceding years (Total 9 years).
    • Conditions: Same transaction, timely return & 92E report filed in rollback years, must apply for all eligible rollback years (no picking), additional fee (₹5 Lakh).
    • Restrictions: Not allowed if ITAT decided the issue for that year, or if applying rollback reduces income below returned income (can reduce below assessed income).
  • Effect: Requires filing Modified Returns within 3 months from end of month APA is entered into. AO recomputes assessment (within 1 year from end of FY of modified return filing) or gets extended time (24m) for pending assessments.
  • Fees: Tiered based on transaction value (₹10L up to 100Cr, ₹15L 100-200Cr, ₹20L >200Cr).
  • Amalgamation/Demerger: Surviving/demerged original entity can get rollback; newly formed entity cannot.

5. Secondary Adjustment (Sec 92CE)

Arises after a Primary Adjustment (increase in taxable income due to ALP application).

  • Condition: Primary adjustment > ₹1 Crore AND relates to AY 2017-18 onwards.
  • Trigger: Primary adjustment arises from: Suo motu adjustment by assessee, AO order accepted by assessee, APA, SHR, or MAP resolution.
  • Rule: The 'excess money' (amount of primary adjustment) must be repatriated to India within 90 days (time limit calculation varies based on trigger - e.g., from AO order date, from date of filing return if APA entered before due date, etc.).
  • Consequence of Non-Repatriation: Excess money treated as a deemed advance/loan to the AE. Interest is imputed annually on this amount and added to assessee's income until repatriation.
  • Interest Rate:
    • INR Denominated: SBI 1-Year MCLR (as on 1st April of PY) + 3.25%.
    • Foreign Currency: 6-Month LIBOR (as on 30th Sept of PY) + 3.00%.
  • One-Time Tax Option: Assessee can opt to pay additional tax @ 18% + 12% Surcharge + 4% Cess (Effective ≈ 20.966%) on the non-repatriated amount. Payment stops further secondary adjustment interest imputation.

6. Documentation & Reporting

6.1. Maintenance of Documents (Sec 92D)

  • Maintain prescribed information & documents (TP Study Report).
  • Mandatory if aggregate value of international transactions > ₹1 Crore.
  • Retain records for 8 years from end of relevant AY.
  • Furnish to AO/CIT(A) within 30 days of notice (extendable by 30 days).

6.2. Report from CA (Sec 92E)

  • Accountant's report in Form No. 3CEB for international transactions & SDTs.
  • Due Date: 31st October of the Assessment Year.

6.3. Master File & CbCR (Sec 286)

  • Part of BEPS Action 13 for transparency of MNE groups.
  • Master File (Form 3CEAA):
    • Overview of the MNE group's global business operations and TP policies.
    • Filed by each constituent entity resident in India.
    • Thresholds: Consolidated Group Revenue (PY) > ₹500 Crore AND (Aggregate Intl Txn > ₹50 Crore OR Intl Txn in Intangibles > ₹10 Crore).
    • Part A: Mandatory filing by due date.
    • Part B: Only if thresholds are met.
    • Due Date: Due date of filing Return of Income (30th November).
    • Maintain records for 8 years.
  • Country-by-Country Report (CbCR) (Form 3CEAD):
    • Detailed country-wise information on revenue, profit, tax paid, employees, assets, etc.
    • Filed by:
      • Parent Entity resident in India, OR
      • Alternate Reporting Entity designated by the group in India, OR
      • Indian Constituent Entity if Parent isn't required to file in its country, or no exchange agreement exists, or systemic failure occurs.
    • Threshold: Consolidated Group Revenue (PY prior to reporting accounting year) ≥ ₹6400 Crore (or €750 Million equivalent).
    • Due Date: Within 12 months from the end of the reporting accounting year.
  • Intimation (Form 3CEAC): Indian constituent entity must inform authorities about its parent/reporting entity at least 2 months before CbCR due date.

7. Specific Anti-Avoidance Rules

7.1. Notified Jurisdictional Area (NJA) (Sec 94A)

  • Transactions with persons in NJA are deemed transactions between AEs, and TP applies.
  • No tolerance band (±3%/1%) benefit.
  • Payments to persons in NJA attract TDS @ 30% or higher applicable rate.
  • Receipts from NJA require assessee to explain the source in the hands of the payer, else deemed income.
  • Note: Currently, no country is notified as an NJA.

7.2. Specified Domestic Transactions (SDT) (Sec 92BA)

  • TP provisions apply to certain domestic transactions if aggregate value > ₹20 Crore.
  • Typically covers transactions between related domestic entities where one enjoys a tax holiday/benefit (e.g., Sec 80-IA, 10AA, 115BAB) to prevent profit shifting.

7.3. Thin Capitalisation Rules (Sec 94B)

Limits interest deduction claimed by an Indian company (or PE of foreign co.) on debt issued by a non-resident AE.

  • Limit: Interest expense deduction restricted to 30% of EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization).
  • Disallowed Interest: The excess interest (above 30% EBITDA) is disallowed.
  • Carry Forward: Disallowed interest can be carried forward for 8 Assessment Years and claimed in future years subject to the 30% limit.
  • Deemed AE Debt: Applies even if loan is from unrelated lender, if an AE provides an implicit/explicit guarantee or deposits matching funds.
  • Exemptions:
    • Companies in Banking or Insurance business.
    • Total interest paid/payable to AEs during the year ≤ ₹1 Crore.

7.4. Avoidance via Asset Transfer to NR (Sec 93)

  • If a resident transfers assets to a non-resident, but retains power to enjoy the income, that income can be deemed income of the resident transferor. (Anti-avoidance measure).

8. Penalties

  • Sec 271AA: 2% of transaction value for failure to: maintain/keep docs (92D), report transactions, or furnish correct info. Also ₹5 Lakh for failure to furnish Master File.
  • Sec 271G: 2% of transaction value for failure to furnish documents/info required by TPO/AO (under 92D).
  • Sec 271BA: ₹1 Lakh for failure to furnish CA report (Form 3CEB) under Sec 92E.
  • Sec 270A: Failure to report international transaction treated as misreporting; penalty @ 200% of tax payable on under-reported income.
  • Sec 271GB: Penalties for failure/delay/inaccuracy related to CbCR filing (daily penalties escalating up to ₹5k/15k/50k per day; ₹5L for inaccurate info furnished).

Note: Penalties may be waived if reasonable cause is shown (Sec 273B).

Key Exam Focus Areas

  • Definition of Associated Enterprise (Sec 92A) - Conditions are crucial.
  • Methods for ALP Computation (Sec 92C) - Especially CUP & CPM with adjustments.
  • Range Concept (Application and Calculation).
  • Secondary Adjustment (Sec 92CE) - Conditions, Repatriation, Interest, One-time tax.
  • Thin Capitalisation (Sec 94B) - Limit, Carry forward, Exceptions.
  • Documentation, CbCR & Master File Requirements & Thresholds.
  • Penalties related to TP non-compliance.
