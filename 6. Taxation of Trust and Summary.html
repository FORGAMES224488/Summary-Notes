Summary: Taxation of Trusts & Institutions

1. Introduction & Definitions

  • Applies to Charitable and Religious Trusts.
  • Religious Trust: Not defined; generally trusts for temples, mosques, churches, gurdwaras.
  • Charitable Purpose (Sec 2(15)): Includes:
    1. Relief of the poor
    2. Education
    3. Medical relief
    4. Yoga
    5. Preservation of Environment (incl. watershed, forests, wildlife)
    6. Preservation of Monuments/Places/Objects of artistic/historic interest
    7. Advancement of any other object of General Public Utility (GPU)
  • GPU Restriction: Trusts with GPU as their object cannot engage in business, unless:
    • Business is incidental to main object, AND
    • Receipts from such business do not exceed 20% of total receipts of the trust in the P.Y.
    • If limit exceeded, exemption u/s 11/12 lost for that year (taxed u/s 13(10)), but registration not automatically cancelled.

2. Computation of Exempt Income (Sec 11)

  • Basic Exemption: 15% of income derived from property held under trust is unconditionally exempt.
  • Application of Income: Remaining 85% is exempt to the extent it is applied to charitable/religious purposes in India during the P.Y.
    • 'Applied' includes both Revenue and Capital Expenditure.
    • Application must be on payment basis (w.e.f PY 21-22).
  • Donations: Treated as income derived from property held under trust, except Corpus Donations.
  • Corpus Donations: Donations received with a specific direction to form part of corpus. Fully exempt if invested in specified modes u/s 11(5). Non-investment or misapplication leads to tax @ 30% u/s 115BBI.
  • Deemed Application (Explanation 1 to Sec 11(1)):
    • Income accrued but not received: File Form 9A, apply in year of receipt or next year.
    • Income received but couldn't apply (any reason): File Form 9A, apply in the next P.Y.
    • Failure to apply later leads to tax @ 30% u/s 115BBI.
    • Form 9A to be filed by return due date (Circular 6/2023 clarifies this, superseding "2 months prior" rule for exemption denial).
  • Donation to Other Trusts: Application allowed for non-corpus donations, but restricted to 85% of the amount donated (Explanation 4).
  • Application from Corpus/Loan (Explanation 4): Expenditure met by withdrawing from corpus or taking loan is not application in the year of expenditure. Allowed as application in the year corpus is reimbursed / loan is repaid, provided this happens within 5 years from the end of the year of expenditure (Amendment).
  • No Carry Forward of Deficit (Explanation 5): If application exceeds income, the deficit lapses.
  • Disallowances Applicable (Explanation 3): Provisions of Sec 40A(3) (cash payment > â‚¹10k), 40A(3A), and 40(a)(ia) (TDS default) apply to application of income.
  • No Depreciation (Sec 11(6)): If the cost of an asset has been claimed as application, depreciation on it is not allowed.

3. Accumulation of Income (Sec 11(2))

  • Income not applied (from the 85% portion) can be accumulated for specific purposes for a period up to 5 years.
  • Conditions:
    1. Specify purpose(s) in Form 10.
    2. Specify period of accumulation (max 5 years).
    3. Invest accumulated amount in modes specified u/s 11(5).
    4. File Form 10 by the due date of filing return (Circular 6/2023 clarification).
  • Consequences of Violation: Misapplication, non-application within specified period, donation to other trust, or violating 11(5) leads to taxation of accumulated amount @ 30% u/s 115BBI.
  • Additional Conditions (Sec 13(9)): Benefit of Sec 11(2) available only if Return is filed on time and Form 10 is filed by the return due date.

4. Conditions for Exemption & Registration

  • Conditions u/s 12A: To claim exemption u/s 11/12:
    1. Trust must be registered u/s 12AB.
    2. Maintain books of account; get accounts audited if total income (before exemption) > Basic Exemption Limit; submit audit report.
    3. File return of income u/s 139(4A) by the due date (incl. belated).
  • Registration u/s 12AB:
    • New Trusts: Apply 1 month before activity commencement -> Provisional Regn (3 yrs) -> Apply for Final Regn (within 6m of activity start / 6m before provisional expiry, whichever earlier) -> Final Regn (5 yrs).
    • Existing Trusts (under 12AA): Re-registered u/s 12AB for 5 yrs (transition).
    • Renewal: Apply 6 months before expiry -> Renewed for 5 yrs.
    • Modification of Objects: Apply within 30 days -> New Regn for 5 yrs.
    • Condonation of Delay: CIT/PCIT can condone delay in application if reasonable cause exists.
    • Cancellation: Possible for 'specified violations' (e.g., non-genuine activities, benefit to specific persons, non-compliance, false info in application).
  • Interplay with Sec 10 (Sec 11(7)): If registered u/s 12AB, generally cannot claim Sec 10 exemptions, except Sec 10(1) Agricultural Income. If also approved u/s 10(23C)(iv)-(via), the 12AB registration becomes inoperative.

5. Circumstances Denying Exemption (Sec 13)

  • Complete Denial (Sec 13(1)(a)/(b)): Income for private religious purposes or benefit of a particular community/caste. (Also leads to cancellation).
  • Partial Denial / Specific Taxation (Sec 13(1)(c)/(d)):
    • Income/property used for benefit of specified persons (founder, trustee, substantial contributor, relatives etc.). -> Benefit amount taxed @ 30% u/s 115BBI.
    • Funds invested otherwise than in modes specified u/s 11(5). -> Amount not so invested taxed @ 30% u/s 115BBI.
  • Taxation u/s 13(10): When GPU trust exceeds 20% commercial receipt limit, or audit/books/return filing conditions u/s 12A are violated.
    • No Sec 11/12 exemption for that year.
    • Taxable Income = Gross Receipts - Allowable Revenue Expenditure (conditions apply). No capital exp deduction here.

6. Special Tax Regimes

  • Tax on Certain Income @ 30% (Sec 115BBI): Applies to income accumulated/set apart in violation of Sec 11(2) or Exp 1 to 11(1); income violating Sec 13(1)(c)/(d); income applied outside India. No deductions/allowances against this income.
  • Tax on Anonymous Donations (Sec 115BBC): Applies to charitable trusts & edu/medical part of mixed trusts. Tax @ 30% on anonymous donations exceeding [Higher of â‚¹1 Lakh or 5% of Total Donations]. The threshold amount is treated as normal income. Does not apply to purely religious trusts.

7. Exemption for Educational/Medical Institutions (Sec 10(23C))

  • Govt Institutions (iiiab/iiiac): Fully exempt if >50% govt grant. No registration needed.
  • Small Non-Govt Institutions (iiiad/iiiae): Fully exempt if aggregate annual gross receipts â‰¤ â‚¹5 Crore AND exists solely for edu/medical purpose (not for profit). No registration needed.
  • Large Non-Govt Institutions (iv/v/vi/via): Exemption calculated similar to Sec 11 (15% + 85% application). Requires approval/registration.
    • Process similar to Sec 12AB.
    • Mutually exclusive with Sec 12AB registration.
    • Approvals under these clauses cease from Oct 1, 2024. New/renewing institutions must register under Sec 12AB only.

8. Exit Tax on Accreted Income (Sec 115TD)

  • Levied when a registered/approved trust (u/s 12AB or 10(23C)) ceases eligibility.
  • Rate: Maximum Marginal Rate (MMR) = 34.944% (30% tax + 12% surcharge + 4% cess).
  • Accreted Income: FMV of Assets - Liabilities on valuation date. (Excludes assets from Agri income, pre-registration period income, and assets transferred on dissolution within 12m). Valuation rules specified.
  • Trigger Events:
    1. Conversion to non-eligible form: Cancellation of registration; failure to get fresh regn after modification; failure to re-register/renew/apply for final regn.
    2. Merger with a non-registered trust or trust with dissimilar objects. (No tax if merged with registered trust with similar objects + other conditions met (confirming amendment)).
    3. Dissolution without transferring assets to another registered trust within 12 months.
  • Payment Due: Within 14 days from trigger event / order. Default leads to interest (1%/month) and penalty (up to 100% tax).
  • Cost base for subsequent transfer of asset on which exit tax paid = FMV considered for exit tax.
