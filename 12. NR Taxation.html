Summary: Non-Resident & NRI Taxation

1. Residential Status

A. Individual (Sec 6(1), 6(6), 6(1A))

  • Basic Conditions (Any one):
    1. Stay in India ≥ 182 days in PY.
    2. Stay in India ≥ 60 days in PY AND ≥ 365 days in preceding 4 PYs.
  • Exceptions to 60-day rule (Only 182-day rule applies):
    • Indian Citizen leaving India for employment outside India in PY.
    • Indian Citizen leaving India as a crew member of an Indian ship in PY.
    • Indian Citizen (IC) or Person of Indian Origin (PIO) visiting India with total Indian Income (excluding foreign sources) ≤ ₹15 Lakhs in PY.
  • Special Rule for IC/PIO Visiting India (Income > ₹15 Lakhs):
    • The 60-day period in Basic Condition (2) becomes 120 days.
    • If condition (1) fails but condition (2) (with 120 days) is met, the individual is directly treated as Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident (R&NOR).
  • Deemed Resident (Sec 6(1A)): An Indian Citizen with total Indian Income > ₹15 Lakhs, not liable to tax in any other country/territory due to domicile/residence, is deemed R&NOR in India (even if basic conditions under Sec 6(1) are not met).
  • Resident Status: Resident if satisfies at least one basic condition; otherwise Non-Resident (NR).
  • Ordinarily Resident Status (R&OR / R&NOR) (Sec 6(6)): A Resident becomes R&OR if BOTH additional conditions are met; otherwise R&NOR.
    1. Resident in India for ≥ 2 PYs out of 10 preceding PYs.
    2. Stay in India for ≥ 730 days in 7 preceding PYs.
  • PIO: Person whose parents/grandparents were born in Undivided India.
  • Counting Days: Day of arrival AND day of departure count as stay in India. Crew member joining/signing off period on foreign-bound ship is excluded.

B. HUF

  • Resident: If Control & Management (C&M) is wholly or partly in India.
  • Non-Resident: If C&M is wholly outside India.
  • R&OR/R&NOR: Depends on Karta satisfying both additional conditions (Sec 6(6)).

C. Company (Sec 6(3))

  • Indian Company: Always Resident.
  • Foreign Company: Resident if its Place of Effective Management (POEM) is in India in that PY.
  • POEM Definition: Place where key management and commercial decisions necessary for conducting business as a whole are, in substance, made.
  • Guidelines for POEM (if Turnover > ₹50 Cr):
    • Active Business Outside India (ABOI) Test: Fulfilled if ALL conditions met:
      1. Passive Income ≤ 50% of Total Income.
      2. Assets in India < 50% of Total Assets.
      3. Employees in India/Resident in India < 50% of Total Employees.
      4. Payroll for India employees < 50% of Total Payroll.
    • If ABOI test fulfilled AND Majority of Board Meetings held outside India => POEM is outside India (Company is NR).
    • If ABOI test fails OR key decisions are actually taken from India (substance over form) => POEM determined by location of key decision-makers (may be India).
    • Averaging: ABOI tests generally use average data of current PY and preceding 2 PYs.
  • Note: If foreign company turnover ≤ ₹50 Cr, POEM guidelines don't apply; generally considered NR unless proven otherwise.

D. Firm, AOP/BOI, Others

  • Resident: If Control & Management (C&M) is wholly or partly in India.
  • Non-Resident: If C&M is wholly outside India.
  • (No R&OR/R&NOR classification for these entities)

2. Scope of Total Income (Sec 5)

  • R&OR: Global income is taxable (Indian + Foreign).
  • R&NOR: Indian income + Foreign income from a business controlled from India or a profession set up in India.
  • NR: Only Indian income (income received/deemed received in India OR accrued/deemed accrued in India).
  • Remittance to India ≠ Receipt in India.

3. Deemed Income

A. Deemed Received in India (Sec 7)

  • Employer's contribution to RPF > 12% of salary.
  • Interest credited to RPF > 9.5% p.a.
  • Employer's contribution to NPS (Tier-I).
  • Transferred balance from URPF to RPF (Employer contribution + Interest).

B. Deemed to Accrue or Arise in India (Sec 9)

  • Business Connection (BC): Income through any BC in India.
    • BC exists if an agent in India (dependent): (i) habitually concludes contracts, (ii) plays principal role in contract conclusion, (iii) habitually maintains stock & delivers, (iv) habitually secures orders mainly/wholly for NR.
    • Significant Economic Presence (SEP): Deemed BC if:
      • Transactions (goods/services/property including data/software download) with any person in India > ₹2 Crore in PY.
      • Systematic solicitation/interaction via digital means with ≥ 3 Lakh users in India.
    • Digital Income: Income from ads targeting Indian customers/IPs or sale/use of data collected from Indian persons/IPs can be attributed to Indian operations.
    • Exclusions: Purchase for export, News collection for transmission outside India, Film shooting (conditions apply), Diamond display by foreign co. in notified zones.
    • (Note: DTAAs often use PE concept, overriding BC where applicable).
  • Property/Asset/Source in India: Income from any property, asset, or source of income situated in India.
  • Capital Gains: On transfer of a capital asset situated in India.
    • Indirect Transfer (Expln 5 to Sec 9(1)(i)): Share/interest in a foreign entity is deemed situated in India if it derives its value substantially (Indian Assets FMV > ₹10 Cr AND Indian Assets ≥ 50% of Total Assets) from assets located in India. Transferring such foreign shares attracts Indian capital gains tax. Small shareholder (<5% holding + no control) exemption available. Valuation date rules apply. Tax is proportional to Indian asset value.
  • Salary: Earned in India (for services rendered in India, even if paid later/outside). Salary paid by Indian Govt. to an Indian citizen for services outside India (Allowances/Perks exempt u/s 10(7)).
  • Dividend: Paid by an Indian company.
  • Interest (Sec 9(1)(v)): Payable by:
    • Govt of India.
    • Resident person (Except if loan used for business/profession outside India or earning income from source outside India).
    • Non-Resident person (If loan used for business/profession *in* India).
  • Royalty / Fees for Technical Services (FTS) (Sec 9(1)(vi)/(vii)): Payable by:
    • Govt of India.
    • Resident person (Except if used for business/profession outside India or earning income from source outside India).
    • Non-Resident person (If used for business/profession *in* India or earning income from source *in* India).
    • (GoDaddy case: Facilitation fees held not royalty).
  • Gift (Sec 9(1)(viii)): Sum of money (>₹50k aggregate) received outside India by NR/R&NOR from a Resident person on or after 05.07.2019.

4. Taxation of Certain NR/Foreign Co Incomes

A. Interest (Sec 115A)

  • From Infrastructure Debt Fund (Sec 10(47)): 5%
  • On specific Foreign Currency Loans/Long Term Bonds/RDBs (issued by Indian Co./Biz Trust by 30.06.2023): 5%
    • Rate becomes 4% if Bonds/RDBs issued Apr'20-Jun'23 & listed *only* on IFSC exchange.
    • Rate becomes 9% if Bonds/RDBs issued Jul'23 onwards & listed *only* on IFSC exchange.
    • Exemption 10(4C): Interest on RDBs issued Sep'18-Mar'19 is fully exempt.
  • From Biz Trust (sourced from SPV interest): 5%
  • On other Foreign Currency Loans to Govt/Indian Concern: 20%

B. Royalty / FTS (Sec 115A / 44DA)

  • If NR/Foreign Co has PE in India & income connected to PE (Sec 44DA): Taxed as business income at normal rates (e.g., 35% for Foreign Co.) on net basis after allowable expenses.
  • If NR/Foreign Co has NO PE in India (Sec 115A): Taxed @ 20% on gross basis (or lower DTAA rate).

C. Dividend (Sec 115A)

  • From Indian companies: 20%
  • From units in IFSC: 10%
  • From Biz Trust (sourced from SPV dividend where SPV paid tax u/s 115BAA): 10%

D. OFO Income from Units (Sec 115AB)

  • Income (Dividend etc.) from UTI/MF: 10%
  • LTCG on UTI/MF units: 10% (or 12.5% if transferred from 23.07.2024)

E. NR Income from Bonds/GDRs (Sec 115AC)

  • Income (Interest/Dividend) on specified Bonds (FCCB)/GDRs: 10%
  • LTCG on specified Bonds/GDRs: 10% (or 12.5% if transferred from 23.07.2024)

F. Resident Employee Income from GDRs (Sec 115ACA)

  • Dividend on GDRs (acquired via ESOP in specified co.): 10%
  • LTCG on such GDRs: 10% (or 12.5% if transferred from 23.07.2024)

G. FII Income from Securities (Sec 115AD)

  • Interest/Dividend: 20%
  • STCG (other than 111A): 30%
  • STCG (u/s 111A): 15% (or 20% if transferred from 23.07.2024)
  • LTCG (other than 112A): 10%
  • LTCG (u/s 112A): 10% (or 12.5% from 23.07.2024) on gain exceeding ₹1 Lakh. (₹1L threshold benefit available here)
  • Surcharge Cap: Maximum surcharge on tax on these incomes is 15%.

H. Specified Fund Income (Sec 115AD, 10(4D))

  • Specified Fund: Cat-III AIF in IFSC or Investment Division of Offshore Banking Unit in IFSC.
  • Taxable Income (Attributable to NR investors):
    • Interest/Dividend from Indian resident securities: 10%
    • STCG (other than 111A) on Indian resident securities: 30%
    • STCG (u/s 111A) on Indian resident securities: 15% / 20%
    • LTCG (other than 112A) on Indian resident shares: 10%
    • LTCG (u/s 112A) on Indian resident shares: 10% / 12.5% on gain > ₹1 Lakh.
  • Exempt Income (Sec 10(4D)): Capital gains on Sec 47(viiab) assets, CG on non-resident securities, Income from non-resident securities, Income from Securitisation Trust.
  • Unit Holder Income (Sec 10(23FBC)): Any income received by unit holders from a specified fund is exempt.

I. Non-Resident Sports Persons / Associations / Entertainers (Sec 115BBA)

  • Applies to NR Sports persons/associations, NR Entertainers (who are also non-citizens).
  • Income from: (i) Participation in India, (ii) Advertisements, (iii) Contribution to articles (newspapers etc.)
  • Tax Rate: 20% on gross income.

J. General Rules for Special Tax Rates (115A to 115BBA)

  • No deduction for expenses allowed against these incomes.
  • No Chapter VIA deductions allowed (Except 80LA against IFSC income, or specified Royalty deductions).
  • No benefit of Indexation or First Proviso to Sec 48 (except Chapter XII-A).
  • These sections are generally mandatory.
  • Basic exemption limit not available against these incomes.
  • Return Filing Exemption: Generally not required if total income consists ONLY of incomes under 115A, 115AB, 115AC, 115BBA, or Chapter XII-A, AND applicable TDS has been deducted.

5. NRI Taxation (Chapter XII-A: Sec 115C to 115I)

  • Applicability: Optional scheme for Non-Resident Indians (NRIs).
  • NRI Definition (Sec 115C): Non-Resident who is an Indian Citizen or PIO.
  • Specified Assets ("Forex Assets"): Shares (Pvt/Public Indian Co.), Debentures (Public Indian Co.), Deposits (Public Indian Co.), Central Govt. Securities - Acquired in foreign currency.
  • Tax Rates (Sec 115E):
    • Investment Income (Interest/Dividend) from Forex Assets: 20%
    • LTCG on transfer of Forex Assets: 10% (or 12.5% if transferred from 23.07.2024)
  • Computation Rules: No deduction for expenses or Chapter VIA against this income. Benefit of First Proviso to Sec 48 (currency fluctuation) *is* available for LTCG, but indexation is *not*. Basic exemption limit not available.
  • LTCG Exemption on Reinvestment (Sec 115F): LTCG exempt proportionately if net consideration is reinvested within 6 months in other Forex Assets or notified savings certificates (Sec 10(4B)). Lock-in period for new asset is 3 years. Formula: LTCG * (Cost of New Asset / Net Consideration).
  • Continuation of Option (Sec 115H): NRI can opt to be governed by these provisions for investment income even after becoming Resident, until assets are transferred/converted.
  • Optional Scheme (Sec 115I): NRI can choose not to be governed by Chapter XII-A and opt for normal provisions.

6. Transition Provisions

  • Foreign Co becoming POEM Resident (Sec 115JH): Rules for determining opening WDV, carry forward of foreign losses (against foreign income taxable in India), accounting period alignment.
  • Foreign Bank Branch Conversion to Indian Subsidiary (Sec 115JG): Capital gain exemption on transfer if conditions met (RBI approval, assets/liabilities transferred at book value, shareholding norms met). Allows transfer of WDV, business losses (fresh 8 yrs), MAT credit (fresh 15 yrs), unamortized VRS etc.

7. Reporting & Penalty

  • Indirect Transfer Reporting (Sec 285A): Indian concern must report details (Form 49D) of foreign entity share transfer if value derived from India.
  • Penalty for Non-Reporting (Sec 271GA): 2% of transaction value (if control transfer involved) or ₹5 Lakhs.
