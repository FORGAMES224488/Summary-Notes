Chapter Summary: Advance Ruling & Equalisation Levy

I. Advance Ruling (BoAR - Board for Advance Ruling)

Provides certainty on tax implications of transactions (undertaken or proposed) involving non-residents or specific residents, mitigating potential disputes.

A. Applicants:

  • Non-residents (regarding their own tax liability).
  • Residents (determining a non-resident's liability they transact with).
  • Notified Residents (currently, for transactions ≥ ₹100 Cr).
  • Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs) (even if issue pending before CIT(A)/ITAT, but not HC/SC).
  • Any Person (Resident/NR) (determining applicability of GAR to a *proposed* transaction - Is it an Impermissible Avoidance Arrangement?).

B. Key Procedural Aspects:

  • Application: Filed online (Forms like 34C), digitally signed.
  • Fees: ₹10,000 for PSU/GAR queries; tiered fees (₹2L/5L/10L) for others based on transaction value (≤100Cr / >100-300Cr / >300Cr). Non-refundable.
  • Withdrawal: Possible within 30 days of filing.
  • Timeline: Ruling pronounced within 6 months of application receipt.
  • Bar on Application: Cannot apply if the question is already pending before any IT Authority/ITAT/HC/SC (exception for PSUs before CIT(A)/ITAT).
  • Rejection Grounds: Pending case (as above), question involves determining Fair Market Value (FMV), transaction is prima facie for tax avoidance (exceptions for PSU/GAR applicants).

C. Board & Ruling:

  • Composition: Bench comprises two members (rank of CCIT or above).
  • Scheme: Operates under the faceless E-Advance Ruling Scheme, 2022.
  • Difference of Opinion: If members differ, point referred to PCCIT (International Tax) who nominates a third member; decision by majority.
  • Ruling Void: Can be declared void ab initio if obtained by fraud or misrepresentation.
  • Rectification: Mistakes apparent on record can be rectified within 1 year from order.

D. Appeal:

  • Aggrieved party (applicant or department) can appeal against the BoAR ruling to the High Court.
  • Time limit: 60 days from communication of ruling (extendable by 30 days on sufficient cause).

II. Equalisation Levy (EL)

A direct tax (levied via Finance Act, 2016, not Income Tax Act) on consideration received by non-residents without a Permanent Establishment (PE) in India for specified services.

A. Scope (Current Focus):

  • Consideration for Online Advertisement Services (including providing digital ad space).
  • Note: Provisions related to E-commerce Operators have been removed/are not covered in this summary context based on the provided transcript. Focus is solely on the advertising levy.

B. Levy & Payment:

  • Rate: 6% of the gross consideration.
  • Payer: The person making the payment deducts/pays the levy:
    • A Resident carrying on business or profession.
    • A Non-Resident having a PE in India.
    (Service must be used for business/profession purposes).
  • Due Date: 7th of the month following the month in which consideration was paid/deducted. (March payment due by 7th April).

C. Compliance & Consequences:

  • Interest (Sec 170): 1% per month or part thereof for delay in payment.
  • Penalties (Sec 171): Equal to the amount of EL for failure to deduct/pay.
  • Penalty (Sec 171): ₹100 per day for delay in payment (capped at EL amount).
  • Return Filing: Annual return (Form No. 1) by June 30th of the following financial year.
  • Penalty (Sec 172): ₹100 per day for failure/delay in filing the return.
  • Income Tax Act Consequence (Sec 40(a)(ib)): If EL is not deducted or deducted but not paid by the ROI due date, the corresponding expenditure (100%) is disallowed in computing business income. (Allowed in year of subsequent payment).
  • Income Exemption (Sec 10(50)): Income subject to EL is exempt from Income Tax for the non-resident recipient.
  • Appeals: Available against penalty orders (CIT(A) -> ITAT).
  • False Statement (Sec 178): Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine.

D. Exclusions:

  • EL does not apply if the non-resident service provider has a PE in India and the service is effectively connected to it.
  • Does not apply if payment is for Royalty/FTS already taxable under Sec 9(1) of Income Tax Act.
  • Applies across India except Jammu & Kashmir.
