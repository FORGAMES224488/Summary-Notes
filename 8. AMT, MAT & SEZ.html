Chapter Summary: MAT, AMT & SEZ

1. Minimum Alternate Tax (MAT) - Section 115JB

Applicability: Applies only to Companies (Domestic & Foreign).

Core Concept: Company pays higher of:

  • Normal Income Tax liability
  • 15% of Book Profit (+ Surcharge & Cess)

IFSC Rate: Rate reduced to 9% for companies that are units in an IFSC deriving income in convertible foreign exchange.

Book Profit Calculation

Start with Net Profit as per Companies Act P&L (Schedule III). Make specified adjustments:

  • Add Back (If Debited to P&L):
    • Income Tax (incl. interest, surcharge, cess), DDT
    • Transfers to any Reserves
    • Provisions for unascertained liabilities / diminution in asset value (Except provisions on scientific/actuarial basis, e.g., Gratuity)
    • Deferred Tax debit
    • Depreciation (Total, including on revaluation)
    • Expenditure related to certain exempt incomes (Sec 10, 11, 12) / specific foreign co. income / Sec 115BBF royalty, if income reduced below.
  • Deduct (If Credited to P&L or otherwise):
    • Withdrawals from Reserves/Provisions (if reserve originally increased Book Profit via P&L)
    • Income exempt u/s 10 (e.g., Agri income), 11, 12.
    • Deferred Tax credit
    • Depreciation other than on revaluation
    • Withdrawal from Revaluation Reserve (up to revaluation depreciation)
    • Brought forward loss OR Unabsorbed depreciation as per books (Lower of the two, unless IBC/Sec 241 case applies - then deduct both).
    • Specific incomes of Foreign Companies (if taxed < 15% normally)
    • Royalty income taxable u/s 115BBF (@10%)

Ind AS Adjustments (For companies following Ind AS)

  • Adjustments for items routed through Other Comprehensive Income (OCI) that won't be reclassified to P&L (except Revaluation Surplus & FVTOCI Equity gains, which are adjusted on disposal).
  • Adjustments for P&L impact of De-merger accounting (distribution of non-cash assets).
  • Adjustment for Transition Amount on first-time Ind AS adoption (spread over 5 years), excluding specific items.

MAT Relief (APA / Secondary Adjustment)

Optional relief available by reducing current year's MAT liability if past years' income included in current Book Profit due to APA/Secondary Adjustments.

MAT Credit (Section 115JAA)

  • Generated when MAT paid > Normal Tax.
  • Credit = MAT paid - Normal Tax payable.
  • Can be carried forward for 15 Assessment Years.
  • Set-off allowed in a year when Normal Tax > MAT, up to the difference between them.
  • Credit reduced if FTC against MAT > FTC against Normal Tax.

Non-Applicability of MAT

  • Foreign Co. resident of DTAA country with no PE in India.
  • Foreign Co. resident of non-DTAA country not requiring registration under Company Law.
  • Companies paying tax u/s 115BAA or 115BAB.
  • Life Insurance Business income.
  • Companies with income taxed presumptively (44B, BB, BBA, BBB).

2. Alternate Minimum Tax (AMT) - Section 115JC

Applicability: Applies to All Assessees EXCEPT Companies (e.g., Individuals, Firms, LLPs, AOP/BOI, etc.).

Core Concept: Assessee pays higher of:

  • Normal Income Tax liability
  • 18.5% of Adjusted Total Income (ATI) (+ Surcharge & Cess)

IFSC Rate: Rate reduced to 9% for units in an IFSC deriving income in convertible foreign exchange.

Adjusted Total Income (ATI) Calculation

ATI = Total Income (as per normal provisions) + Following deductions claimed:

  • Section 10AA (SEZ)
  • Section 35AD (Specified Businesses)
  • Chapter VI-A deductions under heading "C.—Deductions in respect of certain incomes" (i.e., 80-IA to 80RRB, excluding 80P, but including 80JJAA)

Less: Depreciation u/s 32 (if 35AD deduction was added back).

AMT Credit (Section 115JEE)

  • Generated when AMT paid > Normal Tax.
  • Credit = AMT paid - Normal Tax payable.
  • Can be carried forward for 15 Assessment Years.
  • Set-off allowed in a year when Normal Tax > AMT, up to the difference between them.
  • Credit reduced if FTC against AMT > FTC against Normal Tax.

Non-Applicability of AMT

  • Individual, HUF, AOP, BOI, AJP if ATI ≤ ₹20 Lakhs.
  • Assessee opting for tax u/s 115BAC (Individual/HUF) or 115BAD (Co-operative Society).

3. SEZ Deduction (Section 10AA)

Applicability: Deduction from GTI for units in SEZ manufacturing goods or providing services.

Sunset Clause: Unit must have commenced operations by 31st March 2021 (Approval by 31st March 2020).

Deduction Quantum & Period (Total 15 Years)

  • First 5 Years: 100% of Export Profit
  • Next 5 Years (Yr 6-10): 50% of Export Profit
  • Next 5 Years (Yr 11-15): Lower of (50% of Export Profit OR Amount transferred to SEZ Re-investment Reserve)

Export Profit Calculation

Export Profit = (Profit of SEZ Unit) * (Export Turnover / Total Turnover)

  • Profit of SEZ Unit: PGBP income of the unit (Excludes CCS, Duty Drawback, Import License Sale Profit for this formula).
  • Export Turnover: Consideration for exports out of India received in convertible foreign exchange (within RBI prescribed time). Excludes Freight, Insurance, Telecom charges outside India.
  • Total Turnover: Turnover of the SEZ unit (Domestic + Export). Excludes Freight, Insurance, Telecom charges, CCS, Drawback etc.

SEZ Re-investment Reserve (Years 11-15)

  • Amount transferred must be used to acquire new Plant & Machinery within 3 years.
  • Non-utilization / Mis-utilization leads to deemed income.

Other Key Points

  • Requires Audit Report.
  • Business should be new (not formed by splitting/reconstruction - subject to IT exceptions for employee transfer).
  • Plant & Machinery should be mostly new (Used P&M limit 20%, or imported used P&M okay if no prior deduction claimed).
  • Deduction available to new owner for remaining period on transfer (amalgamation/demerger).
