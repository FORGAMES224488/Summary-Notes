Return Filing & Assessment Procedure - Summary

1. Who Must File Return (Sec 139(1))

  • Mandatory: Companies & Firms (incl. LLPs) - Always, regardless of income/loss.
  • Others (Individuals, HUF, AOP/BOI etc.): If Gross Total Income (GTI) before Chapter VIA deductions & Sec 54 series exemptions > Basic Exemption Limit (BEL).
    • BEL: ₹3 Lakh (Default Regime 115BAC), ₹2.5 Lakh (Normal).
  • Mandatory (Proviso Cases - Irrespective of Income above BEL):
    • R&OR Individual: Beneficial owner/signatory for foreign assets/accounts OR beneficiary of foreign asset (unless owner files).
    • Deposits: Current A/c > ₹1 Cr; Savings A/c ≥ ₹50 Lakh (in aggregate, all types).
    • Expenditure: Foreign Travel > ₹2 Lakh; Electricity > ₹1 Lakh.
    • Business/Profession: Turnover > ₹60 Lakh (Biz); Gross Receipts > ₹10 Lakh (Prof).
    • TDS/TCS: Aggregate deducted/collected ≥ ₹25,000 (≥ ₹50,000 for Senior Citizens).

2. Due Dates for Filing Return (Sec 139(1))

  • 31st October (AY): Companies; Persons requiring Audit (Tax Audit or other law); Partner of an audited firm.
  • 30th November (AY): Assessees subject to Transfer Pricing (requiring report u/s 92E).
  • 31st July (AY): All other assessees.
  • Portuguese Civil Code Spouse: Same due date as partner (Oct/Nov) if code applies.

3. Return Types & Procedures

  • Loss Return (Sec 139(3) / Sec 80): Must file by due date (139(1)) to carry forward PGBP, Capital Gains, Owning/Maintaining Racehorse losses. Late filing prevents C/F (except House Property Loss & Unabsorbed Dep.). Condonation possible for genuine delay via application (New limits/authority from 01-Oct-24).
  • Belated Return (Sec 139(4)): File within 3 months before end of relevant AY or before assessment completion, whichever is earlier (i.e., by 31st Dec of AY). Consequences: Loss C/F disallowed (as above), certain deductions (80IA-RRB, 10AA) disallowed, Interest (234A), Fee (234F - ₹5K / ₹1K if income ≤ ₹5L), delayed refund interest (244A).
  • Revised Return (Sec 139(5)): Corrects omission/wrong statement in original (139(1)) or belated (139(4)) return. Time limit same as Belated Return (by 31st Dec of AY). Can be revised multiple times within limit. Original filing date considered for time limits.
  • Defective Return (Sec 139(9)): If return incomplete/incorrectly filed (e.g., missing annexures, wrong form). AO gives notice (usually 15 days) to rectify. Non-rectification makes return invalid.
  • Updated Return (Sec 139(8A)): File within 24 months from end of relevant AY. Allows declaring additional income (cannot reduce income/liability or claim/increase refund/loss). Requires additional tax (25% if filed within 12 months from AY end; 50% if filed between 12-24 months from AY end) on tax + interest. Not allowed in certain cases (search, survey, assessment pending/completed, prosecution etc.).
  • Returns for Specific Entities:
    • Trusts (139(4A)): File if Gross Receipts (before 11/12 exemptions) > BEL. Due date 31st Oct. Late fee 234F + Penalty ₹200/day (272A).
    • Political Parties (139(4B)): File if Income (before 13A exemption) > BEL. Due date 31st Oct. Late fee 234F.
    • Institutions u/s 10 (139(4C)): File if Income (before Sec 10 exemption) > BEL. Due date 31st Oct. Late fee 234F + Penalty ₹200/day (272A).
    • Universities/Colleges (approved u/s 35), Business Trusts, Investment Funds (139(4D)): Mandatory filing.
  • Verification (Sec 140): Signed/Verified digitally (e.g., Aadhaar OTP, Net Banking) by specified persons (Individual, Karta, MD, Partner, etc.). Insolvency professional verifies in IBC cases.
  • Self-Assessment Tax (Sec 140A): Pay tax (+ interest, fee) before filing return. Shortfall adjusted first against fee, then interest, then tax.

4. PAN & Aadhaar (Sec 139A, 139AA)

  • Obtaining PAN (139A): Required if income > BEL (by 31 May of AY), Biz/Prof turnover > ₹5L (by end of PY), for Trusts, specified transactions ≥ ₹2.5L (Resident non-individuals, by 31 May of AY), directors/partners of such entities, Cash Deposit/Withdrawal ≥ ₹20L or opening Current A/c (apply 7 days prior - recent change).
  • Quoting PAN: Mandatory for specified transactions (Vehicle sale >2W, Bank/Demat A/c, Credit/Debit card, High-value cash payments, Property sale > ₹10L, Goods/Service sale > ₹2L etc.). Minor can use parent's PAN.
  • PAN-Aadhaar Linking (139AA): Mandatory for eligible individuals. Non-linking makes PAN inoperative (consequences w.e.f 01-Jul-23). Can be made operative by linking + fee (₹1000 u/s 234H). Exemptions: Residents of Assam, J&K, Meghalaya; Non-Residents; Non-Citizens; Age ≥ 80.
  • Interchangeability: Can quote Aadhaar instead of PAN if linked. If no PAN but have Aadhaar, can quote Aadhaar; dept will auto-allot PAN.
  • Aadhaar Enrollment ID: Cannot be quoted instead of Aadhaar No. w.e.f 01-Oct-2024 for return filing/PAN application.
  • PAN Penalties (272B): ₹10,000 for failure to obtain PAN, quoting false PAN, failure to authenticate.

5. Assessment Procedure - Overview

  • Authorities (Sec 116): Hierarchy includes CBDT, PCCIT/CCIT, PCIT/CIT, Addl/Jt CIT, AC/DC, ITO, TRO, Inspectors (Assessment Wing); PDGIT/DGIT, PDIT/DIT, Addl/Jt DIT, AD/DD (Investigation Wing); CIT(A), JCIT(A).
  • Jurisdiction (Sec 120, 124): Based on territory (PIN code), person, income, or case type. Can be challenged within specified time.
  • Transfer of Case (Sec 127): Possible between AOs, requires OOBH if between cities.
  • Faceless Jurisdiction (Sec 130): Dynamically allocated, primarily managed by NFac (Delhi).

6. Powers of Authorities

  • Discovery, Summons etc. (Sec 131): Power like Civil Court - discovery/inspection, enforce attendance, examine on oath, compel production of books, issue commissions, retain books (15 working days).
  • Call for Information (Sec 133): Power to call for information from any person regarding assessee. Requires Sr. Officer approval if assessment not pending (except Investigation Wing).
  • Survey (Sec 133A):
    • Types: General survey at business/charity place; TDS/TCS survey; Survey at Function/Event (after completion).
    • Power: Inspect books, place marks, inventory cash/stock (cannot seize assets in survey), record statements. Can take books (15 days, extendable with approval).
    • Authorization: Requires prior approval of PCCIT/CCIT/PDGIT/DGIT.
    • Timing: Entry during business hours (Biz place) or Sunrise-Sunset (Other places).
  • Search & Seizure (Raid) (Sec 132):
    • Trigger: Non-compliance with summons/notice OR possession of undisclosed assets (Reason to believe required).
    • Authorization: By Sr. Officers (PCCIT/CCIT/PDGIT/DGIT/PCIT/CIT/PDIT/DIT or empowered Addl/Jt level).
    • Powers: Enter/search any place/vehicle, break locks, seize books, documents, money, bullion, jewellery, other valuables (except Stock-in-Trade), take statements on oath, requisition police help.
    • Deemed Seizure (132(1) proviso): If asset not practically movable.
    • Prohibitory Order (132(3)): Restraint on removal/dealing with asset (max 60 days).
    • Presumptions (132(4A)): Found items belong to person searched; Contents of books are true; Signatures/Handwriting are genuine.
    • Provisional Attachment (132(9B)): AO can provisionally attach property during/within 60 days of search (to protect revenue). Max 6 months, extendable up to 2 yrs total or 60 days post-assessment. Can be vacated against bank guarantee.
    • Valuation (132(9C)): AO can refer seized assets for valuation by VO or approved valuer (report within 60 days).
    • Release of Assets (132B): Explained assets released within 120 days of search completion (after adjusting existing liability) upon application. Undisclosed assets adjusted against final tax liability; balance returned. Interest @0.5% p.m. paid on excess cash returned (from 121st day after search completion till assessment).

7. Assessment Types & Procedures

  • Inquiry Before Assessment (Sec 142(1) - RIA): Notice to file return (if not filed); Call for accounts (max 3 preceding PYs) or information (incl. unrecorded assets with JC approval).
  • Special Audit / Inventory Valuation (Sec 142(2A)): AO directs (with Sr. approval + OOBH) if accounts complex/voluminous etc. Done by CA (Audit) / CMA (Valuation). Fees paid by Govt. Report within time specified (max 180 days incl. extension). Non-compliance leads to 144 + penalty.
  • Reference to VO (Sec 142A): AO can refer asset/property valuation to VO during assessment. VO report (within 6 months) is not binding on AO.
  • Processing / Intimation (Sec 143(1)): Automated processing at CPC. Adjusts for arithmetical errors, incorrect claims, disallowed losses/deductions (due to late filing), audit report adjustments. Intimation issued (mandatory now) within 9 months from end of FY of filing.
  • Scrutiny Assessment (Sec 143(3)): Initiated by notice u/s 143(2) (served within 3 months from end of FY of filing) to ensure no understatement of income/overstatement of loss/excess claims. AO makes inquiries, calls for evidence, passes order determining income/loss.
  • Best Judgment Assessment (Sec 144): Ex-parte assessment when assessee fails to: File return (139/142(1)); Comply with notice u/s 142(1) or 143(2); Comply with direction u/s 142(2A). Requires Show Cause Notice (unless 142(1) notice already ignored). Assessment based on AO's best judgment using available material.
  • DRP Procedure (Sec 144C): Applies to Foreign Co./NRs or cases with TPO adjustment where variation is proposed. AO issues Draft Order -> Assessee accepts (final order in 1m) or files objections with DRP (within 30d) -> DRP issues directions (within 9m) -> AO passes final order based on directions (binding on AO). Appeal against DRP-directed order lies directly with ITAT.
  • Income Escaping Assessment (Reassessment) (Sec 147): If AO has 'information' suggesting income escaped assessment.
    • Procedure (Sec 148A): AO issues Show Cause Notice (with info) -> Assessee replies -> AO passes order (with Sr. approval u/s 151 - Addl/Jt level) if case is fit. Not needed if info is from Sec 135A source.
    • Notice (Sec 148): If case fit (or 148A not needed), AO issues notice requiring return filing (time specified, max 3m from end of month of issue).
    • Time Limit for Notice (Sec 149): Major Change from 01-Sep-24:
      • 148A SCN: Within 3 years from end of relevant AY.
      • If income likely escaped ≥ ₹50 Lakh: Within 5 years from end of relevant AY.
      • 148 Notice: Within 3 years + 3 months or 5 years + 3 months respectively.
      • (Previously 3 yrs / 10 yrs for 148 notice itself).
    • Unlimited Time (Sec 150): For giving effect to court/appellate orders involving finding/direction (e.g., shifting income between persons/years).
    • Assessment: AO assesses/reassesses escaped income + any other escaped income found during proceedings.
  • Block Assessment (Search Cases - Sec 158BA-BFA): NEW PROCEDURE w.e.f. 01-Sep-2024 Search/Requisition.
    • Applicability: Search u/s 132 or Requisition u/s 132A on/after 01-Sep-24.
    • Block Period: 6 PYs preceding PY of search + period from start of PY of search up to date of search completion.
    • Procedure: AO issues notice (with Sr. approval) -> Assessee files return for block period (within time, max 60d) -> Scrutiny notice u/s 143(2) issued -> Assessment determining 'Undisclosed Income'.
    • Undisclosed Income: Income not disclosed previously. Calculated based on evidence found. (No set-off of brought forward losses/dep against this).
    • Tax Rate (Sec 113 context): Flat 60% on Undisclosed Income. (Debate on Surcharge/Cess - Generally considered included in spirit, but Sec 113 currently doesn't specify for this context. Summary follows speaker's view: 60% flat). No 234A/B/C interest or 270A penalty applies.
    • Penalty (158BFA): 50% of tax payable on undisclosed income. Waived if assessee files block return, discloses income, pays tax, and doesn't file appeal.
    • Interest (158BF context): 1.5% p.m. if block return not filed/filed late, on tax payable, from expiry of time allowed till assessment completion.
    • Other Person (158BD): If evidence relates to another person, their AO initiates block assessment for them.
    • Abatement: Pending assessments for block period years abate (stop); assessment done under block provisions. Revived if search held invalid. Pending appeals/revisions continue.
    • DRP (144C): Not applicable.
  • Faceless Assessment (Sec 144B): Applies to Scrutiny (143(3)), BJA (144), Income Escaping (147). Managed by NFac. Cases assigned dynamically to Assessment Units (AU). Verification Unit (VU), Technical Unit (TU), Review Unit (RU) assist via NFac. All communication electronic via NFac. Video conferencing available for hearing upon request. AO can transfer case to jurisdictional AO if needed (with Sr. approval).

8. Time Limits for Assessment Completion (Sec 153)

  • Scrutiny (143(3)) / BJA (144): 12 months from end of relevant AY. (Was 9m for AY 21-22, different limits prior). If updated return (139(8A)) filed, then 12m from end of FY of filing updated return.
  • Income Escaping (147): 12 months from end of FY in which notice u/s 148 was served.
  • Block Assessment (158BE): 12 months from end of month in which search was completed. (For other person u/s 158BD: 12m from end of month notice issued to them).
  • Fresh Assessment (pursuant to set aside by ITAT/Revision): 12 months from end of FY order received/passed.
  • Giving effect to Appellate/Revision Orders: Normally 3 months from end of month order received/passed (extendable by 6m). If verification required by order, then 12 months from end of FY order received/passed.
  • Extension by 12 Months: If case referred to TPO (u/s 92CA).
  • Exclusion Period: Time taken for Special Audit, VO reference, Stay orders, OOBH etc. is excluded.

9. Miscellaneous Provisions

  • Certain Transfers Void (Sec 281): Transfer of asset (>₹10k) during pendency of proceedings (where tax/penalty etc. >₹5k payable) can be declared void by court upon AO's suit. Exceptions: Stock-in-trade, transfer for adequate consideration without notice, transfer with AO approval.
  • Provisional Attachment (Sec 281B): During pendency of assessment/reassessment OR penalty u/s 271AAD (>₹2 Cr), AO (with Sr. approval) can attach property to protect revenue. Max 6 months, extendable up to 2 yrs or 60 days post-assessment. Vacated against Bank Guarantee.
  • Authorised Representative (Sec 288): Can be Employee, Bank Manager, Legal Practitioner, CA, CS, CMA, relative, or person approved by Board.
  • Return/Notice Deemed Valid (Sec 292B): Minor mistakes/defects in notice/order/return don't invalidate it if it's in substance and effect in conformity with the Act.
  • Notice Deemed Served (Sec 292BB): If assessee appears/cooperates in proceedings, cannot later claim notice wasn't served/served improperly/late. Exception: Does not cure non-issuance of mandatory notice (e.g., 143(2)).
  • Rectification (Sec 154): Correction of mistake apparent from record in orders/intimations. Done suo motu or on application. Time limit: 4 years from end of FY of order. If application made (within 4y), rectification within 6 months from end of month of application.
  • Demand Notice (Sec 156): Issued for tax/interest/penalty. Payable within 30 days. Failure leads to 'assessee in default' + interest (1% p.m. u/s 220(2)). Intimation u/s 143(1) is deemed demand notice.
  • Protective Assessment: No specific section. AO assesses same income in hands of multiple persons if ownership disputed, to protect revenue. Tax recovered only from final owner after dispute resolution.
