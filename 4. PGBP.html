PGBP Summary Notes (Profits and Gains from Business or Profession)

Introduction

This head computes income from business or profession. Key structure involves crediting incomes (Sec 28, 41) and debiting expenses (Sec 30-37) to a conceptual Profit & Loss Account.

Section 28: Charging Section - Income Chargeable under PGBP

  • Profit/Gains from business/profession carried on during the previous year.
  • Export Incentives: Duty Drawback, Subsidies/Grants, Profit on sale of DPB/DFRC.
  • Keeman Insurance Policy proceeds received by employer.
  • Value of any benefit/perquisite arising from business/profession (whether convertible to money or not).
  • Remuneration/Interest received by a partner from the firm.
  • Non-compete fees or fees for not sharing know-how, patent, copyright, etc.
  • FMV of Stock-in-Trade converted to Capital Asset on the date of conversion.
  • Compensation for termination/modification of business contracts.
  • Rental Income from Residential Property: Always taxed under House Property.
  • Rental Income from Commercial Property: Taxed under PGBP if letting out is the main business activity (Chennai Properties ruling), otherwise under House Property.

Speculative Business

  • Defined: Contract settled otherwise than by actual delivery.
  • Treated separately from normal business.
  • Loss Set-off: Speculative loss only against speculative income.
  • Carry Forward: Speculative loss for max 4 assessment years.
  • Exceptions (Not Speculative): Hedging contracts (raw materials/merchandise/shares), Forward contracts, Derivatives traded on recognized exchanges.

Section 29: Computation of PGBP Income

Income computed as per provisions of Sec 30 to 43D.

Sections 30 & 31: Expenses on Premises & Assets

  • Sec 30 (Building): Rent (if tenant), Rates, Taxes, Insurance, Repairs (Revenue only). Owner can claim rates, taxes, insurance, revenue repairs.
  • Sec 31 (Plant & Machinery, Furniture): Insurance, Repairs (Revenue only). Allowed for both owner and tenant.
  • Capital Repairs: Not allowed as deduction; capitalized.
  • Tenant's Capital Repair on Building: Treated as 'Deemed Building', tenant can claim depreciation.
  • Rent for P&M/Furniture: Allowed u/s 37.

Section 32: Depreciation

  • Conditions: Asset owned (Beneficial owner sufficient) & used for business (active/passive).
  • Lease: Lessor always claims depreciation (Operating or Finance).
  • Hire Purchase: Hirer claims depreciation from the beginning.
  • Asset Classification: Building, Furniture, Plant & Machinery, Intangibles (Goodwill not eligible for depreciation).
  • Method: WDV is mandatory for all, except power generating units which have an option for SLM.
  • Block of Assets (WDV Method): Group of assets of the same class with the same depreciation rate.
  • Key Depreciation Rates (WDV):
    • Building: Residential 5%, Others 10%, Temporary 40%.
    • Furniture: 10%.
    • P&M: General 15%, Computers/Books/Pollution Control/Renewable Energy 40%, Ships 20%, Aircraft 40%, Motor Cars 15% (30% if used for hiring; special rates 30%/45% if acquired 23/8/19-31/3/20).
    • Intangibles (excl. Goodwill): 25%.
  • Calculation (WDV Block): Opening WDV + Actual Cost of Acquisitions - Money Payable (Sale Proceeds) = WDV for Depreciation.
  • Half-Rate Depreciation: If asset acquired and put to use for less than 180 days in the year of acquisition.
  • Sale of Assets (WDV Block):
    • Entire Block Sold: STCG/STCL = Sale Proceeds - (Opening WDV + Cost of Acquisition). No depreciation.
    • Part of Block Sold:
      • If Sale Proceeds > (Op WDV + Acquisition Cost): Block WDV becomes NIL. Excess = STCG. No depreciation.
      • If Sale Proceeds ≤ (Op WDV + Acquisition Cost): Reduce WDV by Sale Proceeds. Claim depreciation on remaining WDV if assets exist.
  • Additional Depreciation:
    • Eligibility: Assessee in Manufacturing or Power Generation/Transmission/Distribution.
    • Asset: New Plant & Machinery only.
    • Exclusions: Second-hand, Office/Residential use, 100% deducted assets, Ships, Aircraft, Vehicles.
    • Rate: 20% of actual cost in the year of first use.
    • Half-rate (10%) applies if used < 180 days in acquisition year (balance 10% next year).
  • Amalgamation/Demerger/Succession: Depreciation apportioned based on the number of days asset used by predecessor/successor.
  • Unabsorbed Depreciation: Can be carried forward indefinitely and set off against any income head (except Salary) in future years.

Section 43(1): Actual Cost

  • Includes: Purchase Price + Installation + Transport + Trial Run - ITC Claimed - Govt Grant/Subsidy related to asset.
  • Interest on Loan for Asset: Capitalize till 'Put to Use' date; thereafter, claim as revenue expense (Sec 36(1)(iii)).
  • Cash Payment Limit: Payment > ₹10,000 in cash for asset acquisition/related expense is excluded from Actual Cost.
  • Specific Explanations:
    • Asset used for Scientific Research brought to business: Cost = NIL.
    • Stock-in-Trade converted to Capital Asset: Cost = FMV on conversion date.
    • Gift/Inheritance: Cost = Previous owner's WDV (if depreciated) or Cost (if not).
    • Re-acquisition: Cost = Lower of (WDV at transfer) or (Re-acquisition cost).
    • Building previously used for other purposes: Cost = Original Cost - Notional Depreciation for non-business use period.

Section 33AB & 33ABA: Development Accounts

  • 33AB (Tea/Coffee/Rubber): Deposit in NABARD account. Deduction = Lower of (Amount Deposited) or (40% of PGBP income). Deposit within 6 months from year-end or return due date, whichever is earlier.
  • 33ABA (Petroleum/Natural Gas Site Restoration): Deposit in SBI account. Deduction = Lower of (Amount Deposited) or (20% of PGBP income). Deposit by year-end.
  • Common Points: Funds to be used for specified purposes. Mis-utilization/Non-utilization in withdrawal year leads to deemed income. 8-year lock-in for assets acquired. Audit mandatory.

Section 35: Scientific Research Expenditure

  • In-house Research:
    • During Business: Revenue Exp 100%; Capital Exp (excl. Land) 100%.
    • Before Commencement (3 yrs prior): Revenue (Salary & Material only, excl. perks) 100%; Capital Exp (excl. Land) 100%. Deduction in the year business commences.
  • Donation to Outsiders (Approved Institutions - RAHUL ICU, IITs, etc.): 100% deduction for Scientific/Social/Statistical research.
  • Unabsorbed Capital Expenditure: Treated like unabsorbed depreciation (unlimited carry forward, set-off against any head except Salary).
  • Condition 35(1A): Donee institution must furnish donor details/certificate by May 31st for deduction.

Other Amortization Sections (35ABB, 35ABA, 35D, 35DD, 35DDA, 35E)

  • 35ABB/ABA (Telecom License/Spectrum): Amortize cost over license/spectrum life, starting from payment year (if business exists) or commencement year. Sale results in PGBP income/loss or Capital Gain.
  • 35CCC/CCD (Agri Extension/Skill Dev): 100% deduction (Rev/Cap) excl. Land/Building. (CCD for Companies only).
  • 35D (Preliminary Exp): Residents only. For new business/extension/new unit. Deduction = Lower of (Actual Exp) or (5% of Cost of Project/Capital Employed). Amortized over 5 years. Audit in 1st year.
  • 35DD (Amalgamation/Demerger Exp): Indian Companies only. Amortized over 5 years.
  • 35DDA (VRS Exp): All Assessees. Amortized over 5 years.
  • 35E (Mineral Prospecting): Exp (in commencement year + 4 prior years) amortized over 10 years, limited to PGBP of that mine. Audit required.

Section 35AD: Specified Businesses

  • Optional scheme offering 100% deduction on Capital Expenditure (excl. Land, Goodwill, Financial Instruments) for 14 specified businesses (e.g., Cold Chain, Warehouse (Agri/Sugar), Pipelines, 2-star+ Hotels, 100+ bed Hospitals, Fertilizer Production, Infrastructure Facility).
  • Conditions: New business (not split-up/reconstruction), mostly new P&M (20% second-hand allowed, imports treated as new).
  • Cash payment > ₹10,000 for CapEx disallowed.
  • No depreciation if 35AD claimed.
  • Loss Treatment: Set-off only against other specified business income. Unlimited carry forward.
  • Asset Transfer Restriction: Asset must be used exclusively for specified business for 8 years from acquisition. If transferred to non-specified use within 8 years, deduction reversed (less notional dep) as deemed income, and asset added to non-specified block at (Cost - Notional Dep).
  • Audit mandatory.

Section 36: Other Deductions

  • Insurance premium for stock-in-trade.
  • Health insurance premium for employees (paid otherwise than by cash).
  • Bonus/Commission to employees (subject to Sec 43B).
  • Interest on borrowed capital (subject to Sec 43B if loan from specified institutions). Interest for personal purpose/Income Tax payment not allowed.
  • Discount on Zero Coupon Bonds (amortized over bond life).
  • Employer's contribution to approved PF, Gratuity, Superannuation funds (subject to Sec 43B).
  • Employer's contribution to NPS (Limit: Lower of Actual or 14% of Salary) (subject to Sec 43B).
  • Employee's contribution to funds (PF/ESI) deducted by employer must be deposited by the fund's due date, else treated as employer's income (Sec 36(1)(va)).
  • Allowance for animals (used in business, not stock) dying or becoming useless (Cost - Sale value).
  • Bad Debts written off (Sec 36(1)(vii)): Must be related to business/profession, previously offered to tax (usually sales related). No need to prove debt is bad. Loan bad debts allowed only if money lending is the business. Recovery taxed u/s 41(4).
  • Provision for Bad Debts (Sec 36(1)(viia)): Allowed only for Banks/PFIs/NBFCs. Limits: Indian Banks (8.5% GTI + 10% Rural Advances); Foreign Banks/PFIs/NBFCs (5% GTI).
  • Family Planning Expenditure (Companies only): Revenue 100%, Capital amortized over 5 years.
  • STT/CTT paid on transactions taxed under PGBP.
  • Transfer to Special Reserve (Sec 36(1)(viii)): For specified financial corporations. Limit based on amount transferred, % of PGBP, and % of capital/reserves less opening balance.
  • Mark-to-Market Losses: Allowed only if permitted by ICDS.

Section 37: General Deductions

  • Residuary section for expenses not covered in Sec 30-36.
  • Conditions: Wholly & exclusively for business, Revenue nature, Not Capital expenditure, Not Personal expenditure, Legal (Not for prohibited offense/purpose).
  • Disallowed: Illegal payments, Bribes, Penalties for breach of law (Breach of contract penalty may be allowed), CSR Expenditure (Check 80G eligibility separately, except for Clean Ganga/Swachh Bharat Kosh which are disallowed everywhere if CSR), Ads in political souvenirs, Dividend, Tax/Interest/Penalty on Income Tax.
  • Allowed: Normal Ad expenses, Gifts to employees (reasonable), Customary expenses, Professional fees, Litigation costs, Interest on indirect taxes, Buyback/Bonus/Debenture issue costs (if not preliminary).

Section 40 & 40A: Amounts Not Deductible (Disallowances)

  • 40(a)(i): Payment to Non-resident without TDS deduction/deposit by return due date -> 100% disallowance.
  • 40(a)(ia): Payment to Resident without TDS deduction/deposit by return due date -> 30% disallowance. (Allowance permitted in the year TDS is deposited). Exception if payee includes income and pays tax (Form 26A).
  • 40(a)(iii): Salary paid outside India or to NR in India without TDS deposit by TDS due date -> 100% disallowance.
  • 40(a)(v): Tax paid by employer on non-monetary perquisites given to employee -> Disallowed.
  • 40A(2): Unreasonable payments to relatives/specified persons -> Disallowed to the extent unreasonable.
  • 40A(3): Cash expenditure > ₹10,000 (or ₹35,000 for transporters) in a single day to a single person -> 100% disallowance. (Rule 6DD exceptions exist, e.g., payments to Govt/Banks, agricultural produce purchase from cultivator, certain employee payments, agent payments, etc.). Payment for past liability in cash also triggers deemed income if deduction was claimed earlier.
  • 40A(7): Provision for Gratuity disallowed (unless for liability already due or contribution to approved fund).

Section 43B: Deductions Only on Actual Payment

  • Certain expenses allowed only if paid on or before the due date of filing the return of income. Otherwise allowed in the year of actual payment.
  • Covered Expenses: Taxes/Duties/Cess, Employer's contribution to PF/Gratuity/other funds, Bonus/Commission to employees, Interest on loans from Banks/PFIs/NBFCs/SFCs/SIICs, Leave encashment, Payment to Indian Railways for asset use.
  • MSME Payments (Sec 43B(h)): Payment to Micro & Small Enterprises must be made within the time limit specified in Sec 15 of the MSME Act (i.e., agreed date up to max 45 days, or 15 days if no agreement). If not paid within MSME Act timeline, deduction allowed only in the year of actual payment (NOT return due date).
  • Conversion of interest into loan/debentures is NOT considered actual payment.

Section 43CB: Revenue Recognition (Contracts)

  • Construction Contracts: Recognize revenue using Percentage of Completion Method (POCM).
  • Service Contracts: Generally POCM. Exceptions:
    • Duration ≤ 90 days: Project Completion Method.
    • Indeterminate acts over time: Straight Line Method.
  • Retention money must be included in contract revenue.
  • Incidental income (interest, dividend) not reduced from contract cost.

Section 41: Deemed Income

  • 41(1): Remission/Cessation of a trading liability previously allowed as deduction.
  • 41(2): Balancing Charge on sale of SLM assets (Power units).
  • 41(3): Sale of Scientific Research Asset (Lower of Sale Price or Deduction claimed).
  • 41(4): Recovery of Bad Debts previously allowed as deduction.

Section 44AA & 44AB: Books & Audit

  • 44AA: Prescribes maintenance of books for specified professions and other businesses based on income/turnover limits.
  • 44AB (Tax Audit): Mandatory if:
    • Business Turnover > ₹1 Cr (Limit is ₹10 Cr if cash receipts AND cash payments are ≤ 5% of total).
    • Profession Gross Receipts > ₹50 L.
    • Claiming lower profits under Sec 44AD, 44ADA, 44AE, 44BB, 44BBB (and income > basic exemption limit for AD/ADA).
  • Audit Report Due Date: One month prior to return filing due date (usually Sep 30th).
  • Penalty for non-compliance (Sec 271B): Lower of (0.5% of turnover/gross receipts) or ₹1,50,000.

Presumptive Taxation Schemes (Sec 44AD, 44ADA, 44AE)

  • 44AD (Businesses): For Resident Individuals, HUFs, Firms (excl. LLPs). Turnover ≤ ₹2 Cr (Limit ₹3 Cr if cash receipts ≤ 5%). Income presumed @ 8% (or 6% for turnover received digitally by return due date). Option to declare higher income. Declaring lower requires audit if income > basic exemption. 5-year restriction if opting out after opting in.
  • 44ADA (Professions): For Resident Individuals, Firms (excl. LLPs) in specified professions. Gross Receipts ≤ ₹50 L (Limit ₹75 L if cash receipts ≤ 5%). Income presumed @ 50%. Option to declare higher income. Declaring lower requires audit if income > basic exemption.
  • 44AE (Transporters): For assessee owning ≤ 10 goods carriages. Income presumed @ ₹1,000 per ton per month (heavy vehicle) or ₹7,500 per month (other vehicle), for month or part thereof. Option to declare higher income. Declaring lower requires audit. Partner's remuneration/interest deductible from presumed income.
  • Common Points (AD/ADA/AE): No other deductions (30-37) allowed. Deemed WDV calculated for future. Advance tax payable in single installment by March 15 (for AD/ADA).

Presumptive Taxation for Non-Residents (Sec 44B, BBA, BB, BBB, BBC)

  • 44B (Shipping): NR. Income @ 7.5% of freight (India loaded or India received).
  • 44BBA (Aircraft): NR. Income @ 5% of fare/freight (India loaded or India received).
  • 44BB (Oil Exploration Services): NR. Income @ 10% of amount received/receivable. Option to claim lower profit requires books/audit.
  • 44BBB (Turnkey Power Projects): Foreign Co. Income @ 10% of amount received/receivable. Option to claim lower profit requires books/audit.
  • 44BBC (Cruise Ships): NR. Income @ 20% of specified sum (receipts from passengers). No option for lower profit.
  • No Set-off: B/F losses or unabsorbed depreciation cannot be set off against income computed under 44B/44BBA/44BBC (Specific rule for BB/BBB allows lower profit declaration, implying set-off might be possible then).

Section 44C: Head Office Expenditure (NR Branch)

Deduction limited to lower of Actual expenditure allocated or 5% of Adjusted Total Income of the branch.

Section 44A: Mutuality Principle (Clubs)

Applies to mutual concerns like clubs.

  • Income from general services to members (e.g., subscription): Exempt based on mutuality.
  • Income from specific services to members: Taxable under PGBP.
  • Other income (e.g., bank interest): Taxable under IFOS.
  • Deficiency from general activities can be set off against other taxable income, limited to 50% of such other income.
