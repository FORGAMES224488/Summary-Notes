Chapter Summary: IFOS, Dividend, Buy-back & More

Income From Other Sources (IFOS) - Basics

IFOS is the residuary head of income. Income not taxable under Salary, House Property, PGBP, or Capital Gains falls here.

Key Incomes Covered:

  • Dividends (regardless of shares being stock-in-trade or capital asset).
  • Winnings from lotteries, games, puzzles, etc.
  • Interest on Securities (if securities are not stock-in-trade). Interest on bank deposits, loans, income tax refund.
  • Rental Income from P&M/Furniture/Buildings if renting is not the business. Sub-letting income (if not business).
  • Receipts from Keyman Insurance Policy (KIP) if no employer-employee relationship exists.
  • Interest on enhanced/delayed compensation for compulsory acquisition.
  • Gifts (subject to Section 56(2)(x)).
  • Family Pension.
  • Director's sitting fees (if not an employee director).
  • Salaries/Remuneration of MPs, MLAs, MLCs.
  • Agricultural income from outside India.

Taxation of Gifts - Section 56(2)(x)

Gifts received by any person are taxable under IFOS if thresholds are exceeded, unless covered by specific exceptions.

Taxability Thresholds:

  • Money: Aggregate > ₹50,000 in a year → Entire aggregate amount is taxable.
  • Specified Movable Property*: Aggregate FMV > ₹50,000 (if free) → Entire aggregate FMV taxable. If for inadequate consideration, difference (Aggregate FMV - Consideration) > ₹50,000 → Entire difference taxable.
  • Immovable Property:
    • If free: SDV per property > ₹50,000 → Entire SDV taxable.
    • If inadequate consideration: SDV > Consideration by more than ₹50,000 AND SDV > 110% of Consideration → Difference (SDV - Consideration) taxable. (Check per property).

*Specified Movable Property: Shares/Securities, Jewellery, Archaeological collections, Drawings, Paintings, Sculptures, Any work of art, Bullion, Virtual Digital Assets.

Exceptions (Not Taxable): Gifts received:

  • From Relatives (defined list).
  • On the occasion of Marriage of the individual.
  • Under a Will or by Inheritance.
  • In Contemplation of Death.
  • From Local Authority, specified Funds/Trusts/Hospitals/Universities (registered u/s 10(23C) or 12A/12AB).
  • From an individual to a Trust created solely for the benefit of the individual's relative.
  • Under specified transactions not regarded as transfer (Sec 47: HUF partition, Holding-Sub/Sub-Holding transfer, Amalgamation/Demerger).
  • Specific notified transactions (e.g., Delhi unauthorized colony regularization, shares under Sec 241/242, Yes Bank scheme shares, PSU strategic disinvestment shares).
  • COVID-19 Related:
    • Amount received by an individual for medical treatment of self/family (if spent).
    • Amount received by family on death of an individual from employer (no limit) or others (up to ₹10 Lakh aggregate), subject to time limits (death within 6m of testing positive, amount received within 12m of death).

Consequence for Future Sale (Sec 49(4)): If tax was paid under Sec 56(2)(x) on receipt, the value (FMV/SDV) on which tax was paid becomes the Cost of Acquisition (COA).

Note: Sec 56(2)(x) applies only if the received asset is a Capital Asset for the recipient.

Related Provisions for Property Transfers

  • Section 43CA: PGBP equivalent of 50C. Applies when immovable property held as stock-in-trade is transferred for less than SDV. If SDV > 110% of consideration, SDV is deemed Full Value of Consideration for PGBP computation.
  • Section 50C: Applies when immovable property held as capital asset is transferred for less than SDV. If SDV > 110% of consideration, SDV is deemed Full Value of Consideration for Capital Gains computation.
  • Date for SDV: Date of registration, or date of agreement if part payment made by specified modes on or before agreement date.

Compensation & Deductions/Disallowances (IFOS)

  • Sec 56(2)(xi): Compensation received by any person (other than from employer/former employer) in connection with termination or modification of employment/business contract is taxable under IFOS.
  • Sec 57 - Deductions:
    • Dividend/MF Income: Only interest on loan taken to acquire shares/units is deductible, capped at 20% of such income.
    • Family Pension: Deduction of 1/3rd of pension or ₹15,000 p.a. (₹25,000 p.a. if opted for 115BAC), whichever is lower. (Fully exempt for specified defence personnel/gallantry awardees).
    • Interest on Compulsory Acquisition Compensation: Taxable in year of receipt, with a 50% standard deduction.
    • Any other revenue expenditure incurred wholly and exclusively for earning IFOS income.
  • Sec 58 - Disallowances: Personal expenses, interest/salary paid outside India without TDS, 30% of payment to resident without TDS, cash payments > ₹10,000, any expenditure against winnings.

Key Case Laws Summarized

  • Manjushree & Co.: Lottery winning income is taxable u/s 115BB @ 30%, even if arises from business stock (unsold ticket).
  • Dr. Ranjan Pai: Receipt of bonus shares is not income/gift taxable u/s 56(2)(x).
  • Shree Rama Multi-Tech: Interest earned on IPO application money kept in separate bank account (statutory requirement) is adjusted against share issue expenses, not taxed as IFOS.
  • Vir Vikram Ved: Repairs expenditure by company on premises taken on rent from a shareholder (director) is not a deemed dividend u/s 2(22)(e).

Dividend & Deemed Dividend

Basics

  • Dividend Taxability: Always taxable under IFOS in the hands of the shareholder at applicable slab rates (surcharge on dividend tax capped at 15%).
  • Timing: Final Dividend taxable in year of declaration (AGM); Interim/Deemed Dividend taxable in year of receipt/distribution/payment.
  • Company Types:
    • Indian Co.: Incorporated in India.
    • Domestic Co.: An Indian Co., or a Foreign Co. making prescribed arrangements for dividend declaration/payment in India.
    • Foreign Co.: Incorporated outside India.

Deemed Dividend - Section 2(22)

Certain distributions are deemed as dividend, taxable to the extent of accumulated profits (Acc Profit).

  • (a) Distribution of Assets: To the extent of Acc Profit (whether capitalized or not - WCON).
  • (b) Distribution of Debentures/Deposit Certificates/Bonus Shares to Preference SH: To the extent of Acc Profit (WCON).
  • (c) Distribution on Liquidation: To the extent of Acc Profit on the date of liquidation.
  • (d) Distribution on Capital Reduction: To the extent of Acc Profit (WCON).
  • (e) Loan/Advance by a Closely-Held Co.: To a shareholder holding >=10% voting power, or to a concern where such SH has substantial interest (>=20%), or for the benefit of such SH. Taxable to the extent of Acc Profit (NOT WCON).
    • Exceptions: Loan by a company in money lending business (substantial part) in ordinary course; Loan subsequently set-off against declared dividend.
  • (f) Consideration paid on Buy-back of shares (From Oct 1, 2024): Entire consideration treated as deemed dividend.

Exclusions from Dividend: Payment on buy-back u/s 68 (until Sep 30, 2024), distribution in demerger to resulting co. shareholders, payment to Preference SH under 2(22)(c) & (d).

Section 80M - Inter-corporate Dividend Deduction

A Domestic Company receiving dividends from another Domestic Co., Foreign Co., or Business Trust gets a deduction under Chapter VI-A.

Deduction Amount: Lower of:

  1. Dividend received (net of Sec 57 interest expense, if any).
  2. Dividend distributed by the recipient company up to the 'due date' (one month prior to its ITR filing date).

Liquidation

Tax Treatment for Shareholder:

  1. Deemed Dividend (IFOS) u/s 2(22)(c): Distribution received, to the extent of company's accumulated profits on liquidation date.
  2. Capital Gains:
    • Full Value of Consideration = Money received + FMV of Assets received - Deemed Dividend taxed u/s 2(22)(c).
    • Less: Cost/Indexed Cost of Acquisition of shares.
    • Period of Holding: Up to the date of liquidation. Taxable in the year of receipt of distribution.

Buy-back of Shares (Domestic Company)

Regime until Sep 30, 2024 (Sec 115QA)

  • Tax on Company: Company pays additional income tax @ 23.296% (approx) on distributed income, calculated as (Buy-back Price - Issue Price). Tax payable within 14 days.
  • Tax on Shareholder: Income is Exempt u/s 10(34A).

Regime from Oct 1, 2024 (New Sec 2(22)(f) & CG)

  • Tax on Company: No tax under Sec 115QA.
  • Tax on Shareholder:
    • IFOS: Entire Buy-back Price is treated as Deemed Dividend u/s 2(22)(f), taxable at slab rates.
    • Capital Gains: Full Value of Consideration = NIL. Less: COA/ICOA of shares. Result is a Capital Loss (STCL/LTCL).

Issue Price: Generally amount received by company including premium, adjusted for prior capital returns. Specific rules apply for ESOPs, amalgamation, demerger, conversion of firm etc., bonus shares (Nil). FIFO method for demat shares.

Amalgamation & Demerger

Amalgamation

  • Conditions: All assets/liabilities of amalgamating co. transfer; >=75% shareholders (by value) of amalgamating co. become shareholders of amalgamated co.
  • Tax Implications:
    • Transfer of assets by amalgamating co. not taxable (Sec 47).
    • Allotment of shares by amalgamated co. to shareholders of amalgamating co. not taxable (Sec 47).
    • COA/POH for assets (in hands of amalgamated co.) & shares (in hands of shareholder) relates back to original acquisition by previous owner.
    • Unabsorbed depreciation & business losses (non-speculative) of amalgamating co. can be carried forward by amalgamated co. for 8 fresh years.

Demerger

  • Conditions: Transfer of an undertaking's assets/liabilities at book value, as a going concern; share issuance to demerged co. shareholders (>=75% by value) proportionately; share consideration only.
  • Tax Implications:
    • Transfer of undertaking by demerged co. not taxable (Sec 47).
    • Allotment of shares by resulting co. to shareholders of demerged co. not taxable (Sec 47).
    • COA of shares in Resulting Co. = COA(Demerged) * (Net Assets Transferred / Net Worth of Demerged Co.).
    • COA of shares in Demerged Co. = Original COA - COA allocated to Resulting Co. shares.
    • POH includes period held in demerged co.
    • Unabsorbed depreciation & business losses attributable to the undertaking transfer to the resulting co., can be carried forward for the remaining unexpired period.

Bonus Stripping - Section 94(8)

Applies if:

  1. Units/Securities acquired within 3 months prior to Record Date for bonus.
  2. Bonus units/securities received.
  3. Original units/securities sold within 9 months after Record Date, while holding bonus units/securities.

Consequence: Loss arising from sale of original units/securities is ignored to the extent of bonus units held. Such ignored loss is deemed Cost of Acquisition for the bonus units/securities held.
